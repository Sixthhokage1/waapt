    If it helps, as to what the Vow entails...

    • Pokemon cannot harm humans in any way unless provoked or ordered to do so by their own Trainers. In return, humans are expected to treat Pokemon with dignity and respect lest they invoke the "provoked" bit.

    • Pokemon and humans should work together rather than be divided.

    • If you break the Vow - especially on a large scale like mons genociding humans or vice-versa - You're gonna have a bad time, especially since at said large scale the Legendaries get involved, and that gets ugly for the offending party FAST, as certain groups of mons in-RP have found out the hard way.

    Basically it's a very rough Pokeverse equivalent of the Three Laws Of Robotics.

    But anyone feel to clarify/correct me here.

    Er, though, on the backstory thing before I head off... running away from home because of it reads kind of weird to me. Like it feels like it's taken from an outsider's perspective in a place where an outsider's perspective is really dissonant.

    I guess... Lavender Town is generally known as 'that town with the ghosts' outside of the town and... it isn't really undeserved. But from the perspective of someone who was born and raised there... it's going to be a bit more nuanced. Like, for someone who's afraid of Ghost-types, he'd know where Ghost-types were more likely to appear (Pokemon Tower before it was converted into the Radio Tower, maybe a Ghost-type associated store district, anywhere at night, and such) and avoid those places like the plague. He might see it as kind of 'That thing that everybody but me likes' (Which isn't to say there aren't going to be some people from Lavender who are neutral or apathetic to Ghosts like him, Morgan being an example, even if her player's retired now.) And I guess, unless his parents are super fond of Ghost-types themselves and never noticed their son was afraid of them, I'd feel like home would be a comforting place. (Though, that might just be me being a homebody.)

    Anyway, I guess, if you weren't already and I just misinterpreted, try and think of it from that perspective? Like, if he ran away, was it from something that was more 'the last straw' that led him to feel he couldn't come home (and is there a reason he went to the dangerous tunnel to Cerulean instead of the closer Saffron or the further away, but probably less treacherous Fuschia?), or maybe try and handle it in a way that'd make sense from where he's coming from, but still make him feel guilty about being afraid of Ghost-types?

    But before that for the things you're not sure about.

    For the first bit, with how I phrased things, that motivation might take things in a more 'be myself or fit in' kind of thing, so... I guess it'd be better to leave the end decision up to you. Ask any questions you may have if you're having trouble deciding, though.

    For running away... if you do it, there are two ways to take that that come to mind.

    • A specific event exacerbates his fear to the point where he goes somewhere where no one could find him (but probably still kind of close by) because he's too afraid to do anything else. When he either is feeling fine enough to come out or someone finds him where he's been hiding, he realizes made people worry and feels badly about that and wants to get over his fear so he doesn't get into situations like that again. [Could also have happened more than once, maybe.]

    • Either due to a specific event or some sort of slow build up, he's feeling extremely isolated from those around him to the point he doesn't really consider Lavender Town his home anymore and runs away. From there, some events might lead to him having a change of heart and returning.

    I don't know if the latter is what you're going for from what you've told me and him getting Ghost-types after that would come off as... kind of weird (and it seems like a thing it'd be more interesting to write out than just see the aftereffects, but that's me), I think, so maybe consider something more like the former. There might be another way, but those seem like good ways to handle it.

    You might also be able to take it in an entirely different direction than the ones I mentioned, but hopefully that helps.
