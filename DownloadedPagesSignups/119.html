    2962 Umbramatic1st May 2016 10:00:53 PM from WAAPT Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: A teenager in love
    Marry me, Keith-senpai
    OK so Crow and I talked a bit and felt a "problem-y newbie signup cliches to avoid" list going a bit more specific than what's elaborated on in the Essays was likely a good idea, and whipped up a tentative one now in the OP:

    However, remember that we reserve the right to accept or reject any player, and give a reason as to why for the latter. If you want to avoid this, here are some common and/or notable newbie mistakes and why you should steer clear of them beyond or expanded upon what is stated in the Essays:

    • CHARACTERS WITH CREEPY FETISHES AS CENTRAL PARTS OF THEIR PERSONALITIES: This is decidedly against this RP's rules, RP board rules, and TVT rules in general. Please keep sexual stuff PG at worst in signups.

    • Non-human, non-Pokemon characters: It's said in the Tone Essay but ignored often enough to be worth repeating that your characters should be some variety of humans and Pokemon. We have strict rules on sapient beings that are neither and have had severe problems when players have tried to spring robots and such on us before so please do not do this. We have a huge cast of fucked-up perfectly good monkeys with quirks both natural and supernatural as well as several hundred species of collectible murderbeasts who are much the same; we have no need for androids or Xenomorphs or homunculi or demons unless you know how to work them into the setting - which you are guaranteed NOT to if you're just starting out.

    • On a related note, characters tailored more for a non-Pokemon setting and/or ignoring what sort of Pokemon setting this is: This is a Pokemon RP. Flavor from other works of fiction should be mild and homage-y and/or have little effect on how your characters function as a Pokemon trainer and team. Also, we are not a grimderp creepypasta or Nuzlocke fic where everyone's trying to kill each other.

    • Working in worldbuilding elements like new organizations, moves, etc. in from the get-go: You'll need to understand and work with the RP's setting before doing anything like this, especially if it's potentially plot-derailing. Stick with canon to start out, please.
      • Additionally, do not join solely because you want to play out a story idea you can't manage work into another RP or turn into a fanfic.Learn to put the words on paper and put them out yourself if that's the case - it takes learning, but you gotta be willing to learn.

    • Antagonistic P Cs: Having a PC with former ties to a villainous organization is A-OK (though Cipher and Flare have complications you'll need to work around and Rocket's slightly overused), but new P Cs actively opposed to the main group of P Cs don't work out very well, and villains are best left off as NP Cs introduced later.
      • On that note, having your PC be chased by a mysteriously shady figure as part of their backstory is a huge I'M OUT TO CAUSE TROUBLE sign over your head and is massively cliche for newbie signups, so don't do it.

    • Cliche-for-species mon personalities: While less vital than with PC ethnicity, try to avoid falling back on stereotypes. For example, Lucario, a mon that often falls victim to this in newbie signups: Instead of just going with the default "super-noble and super-loyal to their Trainer" archetype, try to be more creative; maybe your character's Lucario has a fair amount of Jerk Jock tendencies instead, or is crazy into video games, or a chill dude who's a surprisingly good mentor figure, or a highly dramatic master chef, or names and styles themselves after a fictional character from another franchise, anything! In this RP, Pokemon of all sorts are pretty much people, and people aren't all the same so they shouldn't be either.
    • At the same time don't create flat inverse stereotypes solely for the sake of avoiding this, that's just hacky.
    • Characters with large amounts of wealth and/or influential families: This has historically been abused heavily by newbies, so avoid, please.

    • Defying/excessive arguing against/over-the-top loopholing of any of these or anything else the existing members criticize your signup for: Please, you're new, we've been here a while, if we have a problem you need to properly fix it. Fighting the existing playerbase over wanting something that causes problems kept in your signup, ignoring their requests to remove it, or attempting to abuse workarounds to keep it in gets you nowhere but out.

    • Godmodding of any sort in the signup test: The point of the test is to test-run your writing skills, not your in-universe skill as a Trainer. Excessive dodging, mon durability, etc. helps neither.

    Feel free to send me suggestions for more.
