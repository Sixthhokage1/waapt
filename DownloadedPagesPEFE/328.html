    Volcanion

    Morphs [Oak Catalog #]

    • Volcanion [721]

    Physical Description

    Volcanion is a large quadruped creature, with a rocky reddish-brown hide covering most of its body. Its legs are thick and possess three orange claws each, as well as a pore on each leg - from which steam is expelled from Volcanion's body. All legs have a black "thigh", though while the hind legs connect directly to the body, the front legs have unusual "ball joints" topping them. The creature's body is divided into segments, each oval in shape and connected by a black waist. The back segment has its hind legs and a red tail topped with a black edge. The front segment is considerably larger and has two orange diamond-shaped protrusions just below the head. Said head is attacheed by a black neck and is covered in the same dull hide as most of its body, save for its lower jaw which is orange. Two ears protrude from the top of the head, and orange markings are located above its eyes, giving off the illusion of eyebrows. These, along with spiky protrusions on Volcanion's upper and lower jaws (the former's point downwards, the latter's point outwards), eye markings resembling eye makeup, a spiky "goatee" and white teeth, intensify the creature's near-constant scowl and ferocious appearance.

    The most notable aspect of Volcanion are the arms attached to its front section, used in battle to launch its powerful attacks and clasped into a ring when not in use. Each arm is divided into three segments, each separated by a bright blue line. Each segment has a steam vent protruding outwards and a bright blue circle on the front showcasing Volcanion's water content. The top segments have three orange claws, and their palms have three blue "flaps" that open up whenever Volcanion uses an attack.

    While much of the creature's internal biology remains unknown, a theorised explanation for its ability to instantly generate steam exists. The theory goes as such - Volcanion ingests a large amount of water and stores it in a sac, as with most Water-type Pokémon. However, when generating steam, some of this water is sent to a unique organ that generates an extremely high temperature and vaporises the water instantly, at which point the gas is forcefully released from Volcanion via one of its vents. This organ, if it exists, is something unheard of to the current understanding of Pokémon physiology, and its unique properties combined with Volcanion's use of water attacks have lead those who study it to believe that it makes Volcanion the world's first, and so far only, Fire/Water-type Pokémon.

    Lore and History

    Volcanion is said to dwell in mountain ranges far away from mankind. Although it appears reclusive from humanity, it may have benefited it whether it intended to or not. Supposedly, the southern part of Kalos came to be habitable as a result of Volcanion's actions - namely, a powerful steam explosion it caused in the past destroyed an entire mountain range that stood there, allowing humans and Pokémon to move in and build settlements in the surviving plains; for which the former revere Volcanion as a helpful Pokémon akin to the worship of Landorus by Unovan farmers. Indeed, a steam explosion did destroy a southern Kalos mountain range around the popular time frame, and was heard for several miles even beyond the region, but no official explanation has yet been determined. Skeptics claim, however, that the area was "merely" at least partially volcanic and went extinct soon afterwards, with Volcanion thought to be the delusion of people who were afraid of volcanoes one day becoming sentient and mobile. While studes show the lands were indeed once volcanic, it's unconfirmed as to whether it went extinct before or after the explosion.

    Known Sightings

    As an elusive and reclusive Mythical Pokémon, sightings of Volcanion are rare, and it remains a popular subject for hoaxes. However, a few have been known to have potentially encountered it. A Trainer who once found himself stranded in a mountain saw a large Pokémon covered by fog, with powerful arms that emitted even more fog. All he could see of it was its silhouette, before it subsequently disappeared. A girl who happened to be swimming in an underground lake claimed to have seen a large red Pokémon slurping down water from the shore, even using two odd tubes to aid it. Perhaps the most outrageous sighting comes from a Hiker in Sinnoh, who says that he saw Volcanion arrive in a mountain range near Mt. Coronet by using its arms as rockets. More recently, some believe to have seen it interacting with what is thought to be the mysterious robot Pokémon, Magearna, but such claims are hard to prove thanks to obscuring steam.

    Diet

    Volcanion's diet is largely unknown, but it's believed water is a key part of it due to its requirement in generating steam. As a Pokémon that lives in mountainous regions where very little vegetation grows, it is likely carnivorous.

    Abilities

    Volcanion's most well-known ability is that of its steam generation. By ingesting water and instantly vaporising it with a high temperature organ, it can create steam instantly without needing to wait for the water to boil. Among its more well-known uses is to shroud itself and its surroundngs in steam, creating the illusion of a dense fog or mist. This allows Volcanion to remain largely undetected by humans. By utilising low-temperature steam, it can use the moves Mist and Haze to either protect or reset status changes. As far as offensive moves go, however, it only regularly uses steam for one move in particular - Steam Eruption, a move that so far has not been displayed by any other Pokémon. Firing large bursts of steam from its arms, Volcanion shrouds and envelops its target in the searing hot vapors. With the same power as Hydro Pump but apparently greater accuracy, this move has been described as incredibly destructive by those unfortunate to be struck with it, and most Pokémon have come back with severe burns all over their bodies.

    Some believe Volcanion in these scenarios was merely holding back, as at full power it could unleash enough force to destroy an entire mountain range. From this, it is thought that Special Attack is Volcanion's strongest point. One of the trainers who enountered it claimed its arms crushed the ground effortlessly, suggesting Volcanion is also physically strong. The tough-looking hide it has is thought to give it high Defense (as well as protection against its own superheated steam), but its weight and body type give it a speed handicap.

    One of the strangest claims made by researchers is the possibility of its arms being used as rockets to grant it flight. While it has been seen to have landed from what would appear to be flight, the logistics of how Volcanion can generate enough steam to keep itself airborne for long distance flights, let alone get off the ground without devastating the landscape, remain hotly disputed.

    Aside from steam, its unique physiology grants it access to liquid water attacks, such as Water Pulse and Scald (which works as a weaker version of its signature Steam Eruption ability) and hot Fire-type attacks like Overheat and Flare Blitz. As a last resort, it can use Explosion by building up a large quantity of steam inside itself, then releasing it in a wide radius when it is ready to explode from the inside. Curiously, one Trainer attempted to attract the attention of what they thought was Volcanion by using a Starmie's Water Pulse, only for the creature to walk off as the attack harmlessly sank into its body. This has led some to believe that it has the Water Absorb Ability - granting it an immunity to Water-type attacks, and allowing it to re-energise itself and heal any injuries it may have sustained.

    Hazards

    Trainers are not likely to run into Volcanion due to its reclusive nature, but if they must look for it, it resides almost exclusively in and around mountain ranges. While it may attempt to leave as soon as it is found, some have noted that this is because it possesses some degree of dislike/indifference towards humans, and as such confronting one in battle may prove hazardous to the health of a Trainer and their Pokémon. Due to the high probability of Volcanion having Water Absorb for its ability, Water-type attacks are strongly unrecommended unless one has a means of negating its Ability. Ground, Rock and Electric-type attacks are all effective on it. but besides the last type, its Steam Eruption and other Water-type attacks can prove devastating to Pokémon of those types.

    Above all, do NOT try to anger it, or else it may find no reason to hold back its full power. Should you encounter it on top of a national monument, please do not bait it into using Steam Eruption at full power - you will not only have to deal with the immense amount of burns you suffer as a result, but the repair bill will be sent directly to you.

    Other

    The theorised high-temperature organ of Volcanion is cited as an influence on the development of what would become known as the steam engine, with a metal container of water being heated up by a fire underneath it acting as a less-powerful recreation of the Pokémon's internal processes.

    Carnivine Line

    Morphs [Oak Catalog #]

    • Carnivine [#455]

    Carnivine, like many Grass-Type Pokémon, is plant-like in nature. The most prominent feature being its large, venus flytrap-like head. It has two eyes on the top, with no visible nose or ears. The head also has four spike-like growths on the back, which seem to serve no particular function. The head also has several brown spots which also do not seem to do anything. Carnivine are in possession of two leaf-like arms. Despite the flimsy appearance of these arms, they are able to hold objects and can hold a Carnivine's weight as it climbs trees and other such activities. From its small, round, and brown body is a mass of tentacles. Again, despite their flimsy appearance, the tentacles act as the Carnivine's feet, allowing it to walk just as any other legged Pokémon can. On average, and adult Carnivine will be about four and-a-half feet tall, and weigh about sixty pounds.

    Notable Biology

    As noted before, Carnivine resemble venus flytrap; and like those plants, a Carnivine is able to secrete a sweet-smelling liquid from its mouth, which many other Pokémon find irresistible, especially Bug-Type Pokémon. This is how a Carnivine likes to catch its prey (See Diet below for more information), and can snap its enormous jaws with great speed and with enough strength to break human bones. Its leaf-like arms, again while looking weak, are able to lift, at max, its own body weight as it climbs trees. The arms can also be used in a pinch if it needs to defend itself, giving nasty slaps to opponents, though a Carnivine will usually use its monstrous mouth instead. The tentacles of a Carnivine are not just for walking, as a Carnivine prefers to hunt by using its tentacles to hang from sturdy tree branches. There it well hang while leaving its maw wide-open, ready for any Pokémon to fall into its trap. Once caught and being trapped by Carnivine's powerful jaws (usually killing its prey instantly), and it will then secrete digestive fluids on its victim. It can take up to twenty-four hours for a Carnivine to digest its meal, though it depends on the size of its meal. Carnivine are also able to burrow, its tentacles digging into the ground allowing a Carnivine to have only its head above-ground while it will again leave its jaw open and waiting for prey. Carnivine only do this if there are no sturdy branches in an immediate area. It has also been theorized that some Carnivine prefer the taste of mammal Pokémon, and will use the burrowing method so it will likely catch a mammal instead of Bug or Flying Type Pokémon. The head of a Carnivine is covered in chlorophyll pigments, allowing it to feed off of the sun's rays. In rare cases, Carnivine are born with mutant pigments. These Carnivine are born with brown leaves and heads, akin to dying plants. Despite the sickly look, there is nothing (aside from the color) that makes these Carnivine different from the green ones. These Carnivine are prized by collectors due to their rarity.

    Habitat

    Carnivine appear in a few select locations, and are indeed somewhat rare Pokémon. The most common place to find a Carnivine is Sinnoh's own Great Marsh, and is indeed the only place to find a Carnivine in that region. For reasons unknown, Carnivine have started to crop up in Johto's Safari Zone, along with the Ilex and Viridian Forests. This migration is bizarre, as these Pokémon were not purposely introduced to these areas and only started to appear in them in recent years. Carnivine can be found on Route 18 in Unova as well, though preferring to stay away from the island's beaches. Finally, Carnivine appear in Routes 14 and 19 in Kalos, with a higher abundance of them in the swampy areas of Route 14. A Carnivine is a wandering Pokémon, never having a permanent home in the wild, and will indeed sleep and "hunt" at the same time. It is not uncommon for Trainers to see a sleeping Carnivine dangling from a tree, awaiting food to come to it.

    Diet

    Unlike many Grass Type Pokémon, Carnivine are omnivore; eating both plants and animals. Carnivine are indeed more prone to eating meat and bugs, as it will simply consume anything that wanders into its trap. When a Carnivine catches its prey, it wastes nothing. The flesh, feathers, fur, fat, muscle, bone, and anything else of a Pokémon it catches will be melted in its mouth. Aside from Poison Type Pokémon that are dangerous to eat, Carnivine seem to have no problem with what it eats, and will only spit out things that are dangerous to consume. There have even been reports of trainers seeing Carnivine pillaging nests for eggs. Carnivine will indeed eat plants too, though only seems to do so when meat and bugs are scarce. They seem to prefer sweet berries and honey when it resorts to this. Carnivine prefer to wait for their food, and will only look for plants and eggs to eat if it is starving. That being said, Carnivine will rarely attack anything its own size, opting to hunt prey that can fit in its mouth despite the ability to bite through smaller prey if it needed to. Only the most desperately hungry of Carnivine will attack anything bigger than its mouth. A Carnivine can feed off of the sun's rays, but this is not enough to sustain fully grown Carnivine. See Courting and Childbearing for more information.

    Hazards

    Carnivine are not aggressive Pokémon by nature. Indeed, it will only attack other Pokémon or humans if it is starving and near-death or if provoked. If provoked, a Carnivine can prove to be quite dangerous, able to stun foes by whipping them with its tentacles, and of course using its huge mouth to deliver devastating and deadly attacks. That said, if you do not provoke a Carnivine and it is not overly-hungry, it will most likely just ignore you as you go on your way. If you are going through an area where Carnivine should be found, you should keep your Pokémon in your Poké Balls, as most Pokémon are attracted to the scent of a Carnivine. Only those Pokémon that do not feed by conventional means (such as Magnemite or Ghost Type Pokémon) are immune to the smell. One should also keep an eye on the ground, as if you step into a Carnivine's mouth, it will snap its jaws faster than you can react, and will result either in a broken or severed leg.

    NOTE: If keeping a Carnivine at home, it is required by law in all regions to warn your neighbors so they can be wary of their smaller Pokémon and children from going near. It is also required to feed a Carnivine large meals a day, so it will keep its mouth closed for hours on end and not secreting its attractive saliva. It is possible, though difficult, to train a Carnivine to not "hunt" as they usually do, instead relying on its Trainer to feed it.

    Courting and Childbearing

    Unlike many Pokémon, Carnivine have two ways of breeding, sexual and asexual. Carnivine mating season occurs in early Spring, with the Pokémon wandering their habitats for mates. A female will secrete a pheromone to attract males, and it is possible for males to fight over a single mate, usually with the loser sustaining non-fatal injuries. When the mating is done, the female Carnivine will lay eggs after only a month, burrowing a small hole and burying her eggs. With that, the mother will leave the eggs on their own. They will hatch soon after, with the baby Carnivine feeding off the soil's nutrients for several weeks before being large enough to get above ground. As a Carnivine is this young, it only needs the rays of the sun to sustain itself, only starting to be able to produce its alluring saliva as it grows big enough to effectively catch prey.

    If a female Carnivine is unable to find a mate in time, it will release seeds from its body, where she will plant as many as she can find into the ground. These seeds will grow into baby Carnivine who act and are biologically the same as Carnivine that come from eggs.

    Social Structure

    Aside from mating, Carnivine are very isolated creatures. They prefer to be on their own, only interacting to mate or sometimes fight over food when food is scarce. If there is enough food to go around, it is not uncommon to see two or more Carnivine in the same general area as they lay in wait for prey. Even if a Carnivine catches a hearty meal right next to another, the Carnivine without a meal won't react unless it is hungry enough.

    In Human Culture

    Due to a Carnivine's willingness to eat anything small enough, it has gained a negative view in the eyes of many humans. This has reached the point where some neighborhoods and towns have banned its residents from raising Carnivine, fearing for the safety of children and small Pokémon. Carnivine appear in fiction, usually as villains or common mooks in video games.

    Skidoo Line

    Morphs [Oak Catalogue #]

    • Skiddo [672]  Pseudois herbadfectus

    • Gogoat [673]  Pseudois virentiadfectus

    Physical Description

    The members of the Skiddo line are a pair of Caprid Pokémon whom share a Grass typing.

    The juvenile form of the line Skiddo measures in at between 85 and 105cm tall and can weigh between 25 and 40kg. Skiddo is a quadruped and its body is covered in shaggy, brown fur that thins on its legs. Each leg has white stockings from the ankle to the knee and ends in a brownish- orange cloven hoof.

    Its face is covered in white fur except for a brown blaze running down the centre of its muzzle to its nose. A pair of short black horns that extend in opposite directions out to the side. Skiddo also possesses a leafy green mane around its neck and extending down its back to its tail. Despite the leaf like appearance tis mane is actually made out of modified hairs containing chloroplasts.

    Upon evolution into Gogoat it has nearly doubled in size and now measures at between 1.6 and 1.85m and weigh between 85 and 100kg. Like its pre-evolved form Gogoats body is covered in shaggy brown fur, dark brown along its back and lighter at the sides and on its belly. The white stockings on its legs now only cover its ankles and its hooves remain the same brownish-orange.

    Gogoats face retains its white face with a brown blaze down its muzzle however most of the blaze is hidden by the base of the two long, black curving horns that extend over their back. Its face now also possesses black markings around its red eyes. The leafy mane around their necks and down their backs is even thicker and ends at a now slightly larger tail.

    As with all species of Pokémon the Skiddo line has members that exhibit a rare phenotype that alters their colouring. As a Skiddo these members have yellowish horns and noses while their normally brown fur is now a very light tan and their leaf-like mane is a very light green while their hooves a pinkish in colour.

    As Gogoat these members have yellow horns and eyes and a light green mane. The normally light brown fur is greyish while the darker brown fur is instead black and their hooves are now red in colour. Members with this phenotype are highly sought after by collectors.

    Notable Biology

    One of the most notable features of the Skiddo line is their ability to detect the feelings of a person touching their horns. This is accomplished through a weak form of telepathy centred in their horns. In Gogoat this ability has heightened to the point of being able to act as if it and its rider were a single being surpassing even some Psychic types in this respect.

    Another notable feature is their leafy green manes. These manes despite appearing to be made of leaves are actually made up of modified hairs, each of which contain large numbers of chloroplasts allowing the members of the Skiddo line to perform photosynthesis.

    As Grass type Pokémon both Skiddo and Gogoat have the expected abilities such as releasing a storm of leaves, though in this case its their modified hairs from their mane, and strike at foes with vines that extend from within their mane.

    The members of this line are also able to naturally release a shot of seeds to either drain health from foes or as an explosive assault. The members of this line are also among only five Pokémon not of the Ground type able to naturally learn how to cause localised seismic events to flatten the terrain and slow their opponent with Gogoat also able to create more powerful seismic events.

    The two members of the Skiddo line also possess structures within their horns similar to the Haustorial roots possessed by parasitic plants which end in a small needle-like structure that can be extended out the tip of the horn allowing Skiddo or Gogoat to drain energy from their foes using them. This ability had previously been thought unique to the Unova regions Sawsbuck before being discovered in a number of Pokémon found within Kalos.

    Through special tutoring and instruction the Skiddo line can learn a number of other techniques, among them using the chloroplasts within their mane to form a miniature pseudo-sun using a form of reverse photosynthesis, to use their hooves or horns to deliver a devastating blow to the foe that is also able to shatter energy constructs designed to dampen blows.

    They can also learn to use their weak telepathic structures in their horns to project a psychic field around their heads while head-butting the foe or even learn to summon miniature tsunamis on the field.

    There are two distinct sub-groups of these Pokémon. The first sub-group possess within their fur similar structures to those they possess within their horn allowing Skiddo and Gogoat to absorb the energy infused within Grass type attacks and redirect it towards their muscles giving a temporary boost to their physical prowess. Along with the Deerling line, Bouffalant and the Goomy line, the Skiddo line is one of the only lines with this Sap Sipper ability as it is known amongst trainers as a common ability rather than a Hidden Ability.

    The second and rarest sub-group possess an ability that is so far unique to the line; the Grass Pelt ability. The members of this sub-group possess a unique reaction to the energy given off by the environment within the effect of the move Grassy Terrain. This reaction causes the muscles of the Skiddo or Gogoat to compact and become denser increasing their resilience to physical blows. Members with this ability are a focus of study by researchers since neither Skiddo nor Gogoat are able to learn Grassy Terrain by any known means indicating a reliance on other Pokémon to gain a benefit from this ability.

    Habitat

    Skiddo are found in plains and fields most often around the base of mountains, the slopes of which Gogoat make their home. The only known location where wild Skiddo can reliably be encountered is Kalos Route 5 aka Versant Road though a population of Gogoat has been introduced to an attraction at Kiloude City in south-east Kalos.

    Diet

    Both members are almost completely herbivorous eating grass, leaves and other vegetation though they will happily accept specially made Pokémon food from their trainers. Due to the chloroplasts within their mane both members of this line are able to gain energy through photosynthesis.

    Hazards

    There are few hazards in regard to Skiddo and Gogoat as both members have very mild and calm personalities rarely becoming aggressive. The members will usually only become aggressive when their young or a member of the herd is harmed or endangered. It should be noted that due to their placid personality wild Skiddo and Gogoat will consider any Pokémon smaller or weaker than itself under its protection and thus part of the herd and worthy of protection. In domesticated members this extends to other animals on the farm or other members of their trainers Pokémon team along with any children they have close contact with.

    In the event that a Skiddo or Gogoat does attack it will use its powerful hind legs to strike with its rock hard hooves or will charge with its horns to either gore the threat with their sharp tip or to use their powerful neck muscles to toss the threat away. Due to the most common locations Gogoat can be found, namely steep mountain slopes, these tosses are often fatal or at least severely debilitating while goring can likewise be fatal or cause severe damage to internal organs.

    Social Structure

    Skiddo and Gogoat live in herds of around 6-18 individuals though herds of up to 30 have been found. The leader of the herd is decided by a clashing of horns with the victor taking command. The herd will usually consist mostly of females with only 1 or 2 adult males besides the herd leader.

    Courting

    While both members of the line are capable of reproducing, in the wild it is usually only Gogoat that do so. Members of this line are sexually mature at 1-2 years of age though males usually wont mate until they reach peak size at 7 years of age.

    Rutting season begins in late November and lasts until mid-January during which males will rub their horns on trees and shrubs, fight horn to horn, wallow in mud and dirt, smear themselves with secretions designed to attract females and herd estrus females together. Once the breeding ritual has completed the female will give birth in late June and July.

    Once born the Skiddo are protected by the herd, remaining close to their mother until they reach around a year of age when they will depart to the base of the mountain, only returning to the slopes upon evolution or to breed if numbers in the area reach a low enough level.

    Though giving birth to live young in the wild, the added safety and security of captivity often leads to the birthing of less developed young contained within an egg.

    In Human Society

    Skiddo and Gogoat have an extremely long history with human society. Along with Miltank and the various canine Pokémon lines like Growlithe and Lillipup, they are among the first species of Pokémon to be domesticated.

    The easy and relaxed temperament of the Skiddo lines has made them invaluable to humans over the millennia theyve been used. Gogoat fills most of the roles within Kalos that the Ponyta and Blitzle lines filled in other regions. Their powerful bodies made them invaluable for carrying heavy loads over long distances and their gentle nature made them the most useful mounts when speed wasnt an issue.

    Swirlix Line

    Morphs [Oak Catalog #]

    • Swirlix [#684]
    • Slurpuff [#685]

    Appearance

    Swirlix are small white bundles of fluffy, thin sticky string standing on pink three-toed paws with a short, thick pink tail. Their face, somehow visible on top of the fluff, consists of a pair of red eyes, a tiny black nose, and a large maw with a visibly emerging tongue and a pair of tiny fangs that are hidden when shut. Two bulges above the eyes could, in theory, be their ears, as they are marked with pink tips, but this isn't clear on first viewing. An alternative coloring does exist, with the fluff being a very light brown and the pink portions of their body taking on a more chocolate color.

    A Slurpuff has a much more solid consistency, although there is still a small amount of flexibility to their form, much akin to certain sweet pastries. Their body is pudgy and stout, divided at the neck by a wavy pinkish red collar marking; a similar red dot is on the left side of their chest, a pair of stubby arms located just under the collar, and the whole mass rests on a pair of three-toed pink paws. While the Slurpuff's face is almost identical to a Swirlix's, there is the addition of pink 'hair' piled atop the head, with two pink bulges on either side and a dripping, reddish pink sphere atop the whole thing. Slurpuff that evolve from Swirlix with a brown coloration will retain said coloration; their feet and hair will be chocolate-colored, while their collar and head-sphere will be a maroon shade.

    Notable Biology

    Analysis of the Swirlix line has caused quite a stir among pokemon professionals, as they defy many basic laws of anatomy. While the line does have a basic skeleton, their only defined organs appear to be their eyes, nose, and digestive system; literally everything else is maintained by their sticky floof or pudge in a form of 'self-pumping vein' assortment. Furthermore, Swirlix are less hampered by gravity than should be possible; while they do not outright fly or hover, their hang time is greater than other similarly sized creatures. It is presumed this is because of the psychic field that permeates members of the Swirlix line; they do not have a functional brain, but instead seem to have an intelligence mounted in this field itself.

    Even more interesting is their method of evolution. The Swirlix must consume a specific form of enhanced sugar in order to double their mass and generate the pudgier, larger Slurpuff. This type of sugar is not found in nature, but a family of chefs in Cyllage city have developed a secret recipe of pastry they call 'whipped dream' which can induce the evolution; a Swirlix can either eat a large number of these or, for unknown reasons, be traded with just one to induce evolution. The family does have its own line of Swirlix and Slurpuff as a result; the Slurpuff's incredibly sensitive nose makes it a useful baking assistant.

    Due to the Swirlix line's decentralized anatomy and its love for sugars in all forms, the pokemon has a large amount of energy flowing through its floof. They can also release this sugar in large bursts; this sweet veil keeps both Swirlix and any allies in battle from falling asleep due to the constant sugar rush (although a post-battle crash is not unheard of for their allies). In a smaller captive population, however, there is a mutation that prevents this veil from forming; the Swirlix and Slurpuff with this mutation compensate by hyperstimulating themselves if they're unburdened from whatever they hold.

    Habitat

    The Swirlix line is one of the few known pokemon to have originated in the Dream World; at some point prior to its discovery, the Swirlix migrated en masse out of the Dream World into the Kalos region, where they were almost instantly subjected to a predatory slaughter by native pokemon looking for a quick sweet meal. A small population was apparently 'rescued' by a family, who took to raising them in Cyllage city; over time, the Swirlix and Slurpuff became domestic assistants and helped establish Cyllage's cooking industry. Most modern Swirlix members are either owned by a chef of the city or have migrated to the nearby Route 7, presumably to smell the flowers.

    Diet

    Swirlix and Slurpuff require sugar to a large degree, to the point that some people believe Swirlix cannot eat anything but sweets. While this is not technically true, they can get most if not all of their required nutrients from pastries and candies; they do also require a small amount of good cheer to feed off of, however, and will generally find it by baking snacks for their friends.

    Hazards

    Despite their small stature, both Swirlix and Slurpuff have an incredible amount of potential power contained within their frames. A Swirlix is capable of quickly encasing attackers in sticky thread and bouncing off; while a Slurpuff loses this ability, they are capable of surprising feats of agility. The psychic field both morphs have can produce both defensive shields and bright, dazzling gleams; they can also be trained to generate fire, electricity, and even manipulate weather.

    However, despite their potential power, the Swirlix line is extremely nonaggressive and tends to only attack when their food source is threatened. Most of the dangers associated with Swirlix are more the result of their rascally behavior and minor household accidents; Slurpuff are far better trained, but might react poorly to people who degrade their cooking.

    Social Structure

    While there is no true social structure in the Swirlix line, Slurpuff are able to command respect and direct the Swirlix in their home to some minor degree in return for providing pastries and other sweets. Swirlix are easily distracted, however, and usually wander out of the safety of their homes after random sweet scents; this is presumably the reason the route 7 population remains stable even as they are constantly consumed by local predators.

    Courting and Childrearing

    Once or twice a year, there is a great Slurpuff baking contest, where the Slurpuff of Cyllage compete to create new and interesting foodstuffs. This also serves to attract Swirlix from outside the Slurpuff's own home; over the course of the day, Slurpuff will capture Swirlix of the opposite gender and place them in donut boxes or other such sugarcoated containers for storage. After the competition, the Slurpuff will mate with the Swirlix before releasing them; female Slurpuff will later make a nest of eggs in the box, while female Swirlix will simply lay their eggs in randomly whenever they feel the need. Young Swirlix who emerge from nests are considered the Slurpuff mother's servants until they either run away or evolve; Swirlix from random eggs may integrate into these groups or merely run wild.

    In Human Society

    The Swirlix line is scientifically intriguing, as stories regarding their 'creation' mirror the methods of taking Pokemon from the Dream World used in Unova. Indeed, investigation into the Dream World does reveal a remnant population of Swirlix and Slurpuff, but so scattered and rare that it is assumed they are almost extinct in that setting. Most stories prior to their evacuation describe them as being a sort of 'chef's muse' or even being formed from the dreams of bakers; whether or not this is true is unclear, but it would certainly explain their almost impossible biology.

    Modern Swirlix and Slurpuff are typically regarded as mascots for some baking group or another. They don't show up in stories as protagonists, often being relegated to side roles or bit characters if they are acknowledged at all, but they are almost always in some sort of chef-related role. One notable exception is in a local child's show, where a curious Swirlix is always asking questions and getting into trouble through various shenanigans.

    Morphs [Oak Catalog #]

    Lunatone:

    Notable Biology

    Lunatone and Solrock are very interesting creatures, discovered only about 40 years ago. They are psychic meteorites of similar dimensions and makeup: Lunatone is a metre (3'03") in height and Solrock is 1.2 metres (3'11"). Both of them typically weigh anything from 140 to 180 kilograms (310 to 400 pounds).

    Lunatone is a crescent moon-shaped Pokemon, with a pointed 'beak' in the middle of its 'face'. It has two eyes on either side of its body which are large and red with oval black pupils in the centre. On its body are multiple crater formations, possibly from Earth impact. Meanwhile, Solrock is a sun-shaped Pokemon, with an orange circular body and eight lighter-coloured 'rays' extending from its face. Solrock has two eyes on one side of its face, which are usually mostly closed (unlike Lunatone, Solrock has eyelids) revealing its small, red pupils.

    Both Lunatone and Solrock are notable for their psychic powers, which include levitating as their main form of movement and ability to put their opponents to sleep.

    As with all Pokemon, Lunatone and Solrock have 'shiny' forms - rare genetic mutations that change their colour. 'Shiny' Lunatone looks quite similar on its body, but its eyes are blue; 'Shiny' Solrock has lighter 'rays', a red body and greenish-blue pupils.

    Habitat

    Lunatone and Solrock are quite rare, however they can be found in various locations throughout the world due to meteor showers being spread evenly. They were originally discovered at Hoenn's Meteor Falls, north of Rustboro City. They have also been found at Sinnoh's three lakes, Unova's Giant Chasm, and Kalos's Glittering Cave.

    Diet

    It's unknown what they eat, if anything. A Lunatone and Solrock that were kept by the Faculty of Species Study at Castelia University were found to respond to various berries (in particular the Charti, Babiri and Liechi Berries), but why this is isn't known.

    Hazards

    The two of them together have some very powerful abilities. It is common for them to 'subdue' an opponent by hypnotising them, then attacking with full force. While they can be very dangerous, they will not attack unless provoked. As well as their psychic powers, they also have the ability to use Rock-type powers such as 'Rock Slide', which involves a continuous stream of rocks falling onto the enemy.

    Courting and Childrearing

    Lunatone and Solrock are nominally 'genderless', and therefore reproduce asexually. It was seen in the aforementioned Castelia study that the two of them reproduce by removing small amounts of rock from their own body, then incubating them in a safe spot (usually underground) that grows over 4-5 weeks into a new Lunatone or Solrock.

    Social Structure

    Since they are so rare, little is known about their social structure. They can travel both alone and in (mixed) groups.

    Chatot

    Morphs [Oak Catalog #]

    - Chatot [441]

    Physical Description

    Avg. Height: 0.5m (1' 8")

    Avg. Weight: 1.9 kg (4.2 lbs)

    Chatot are medium-sized parrots with a bright blue wings, a green belly with a yellow chest and feet, a collar of white feathers surrounding its neck, and a large, black head with a unusually shaped crest and a pink beak. When a Chatot spreads its wings, the feathers on the underside are the same yellow as its chest. The tail is comprised of a single black feather that, just before the tip, has a rounded segment, giving it the appearance of a musical metronome. In contrast to most parrots, Chatot have three toes in the front and one toe in the back rather than two in the front and two in the back.

    A rare variant has a slightly bluer head and tail, red wings, a navy-blue belly, and a yellow beak.

    Notable Biology

    The tongue of a Chatot is remarkably similar to that of a human, allowing them to mimic speak with great clarity. They can even use the words and phrases they pick up from their trainers in proper context, although sometimes they will simply repeat what they find the most entertaining to say. As such, it is wise to watch the language used around them, as they may decide some obscenities are quite fun to repeat. Chatot are also capable of mimicking the cries of other Pokémon, and will do so to scare or confuse potential predators.

    Chatot are quite musical, and are known to copy songs theyve heard, or even invent their own, swinging their tail to the rhythm as they sing.

    In addition to their vocal talents, Chatot are incredibly intelligent, being capable of solving puzzles, using simple tools, and can manipulate people and other Pokémon to their own advantage. For example, Chatot in the Hoenn region often will drop particularly hard berries and nuts onto busy intersections, letting cars run over them. Once the traffic lights turn red, they will safely go to collect their food, now cracked open by the weight of the cars, and fly off once more when the lights turn green again.

    Wild Chatot can live to be up to 30 years old, and those in captivity can live in upwards of 50 years.

    Habitat

    Chatot can be found in many different environments, as their intelligence allows them to adapt to their surroundings with great efficiency. The regions they inhabit include Hoenn, Sinnoh, Unova, and Kalos, most commonly in coastal regions, though they also can live in temperate and tropical forests, and even in some human cities.

    Diet

    Chatot usually subsist on a diet of fruit, berries, and seeds, though they are also capable of digesting lean meats. A popular strategy amongst trainers is to feed their Chatot meat as a cheap protein supplement in order to promote muscle growth.

    Captive Chatot not kept in an aviary or used in training should have their intake of seeds limited, as the high fat content of the seeds can lead to unhealthy weight gain.

    Hazards

    Chatot is ordinarily a timid, docile Pokémon that would much rather fly away from danger than confront it. Should they be unable to escape, Chatot is capable of producing a screech in excess of 140 decibels; a volume loud enough to cause hearing damage in most Pokémon and people at close range. For this reason, attempting to capture wild Chatot is unadvised without proper ear protection or a Pokémon with the ability Soundproof.

    Chatot are notoriously moody, and even among trusted trainers or other Pokémon, may escalate from being cuddly and affectionate to pecking and biting within a matter of seconds, often for no apparent reason. The frequency of these mood swings can be reduced with proper bathing or misting (which ever the Pokémon prefers), restricting petting to the head and feet, and limiting their intake of fresh fruit.

    Courting and Childrearing

    Chatot sexually mature between the ages of three to four years, and can mate at any point in time afterwards, though they get particularly hormonal during dry seasons. Instead of mating calls or rituals, Chatot tend to take a more casual approach to finding a mate, first forming a friendship with an individual, and propositioning the idea of mating with them once the bond is strong enough. While most of these pairings involve a male and a female Chatot, homosexual relationships are quite common. The pair, regardless of sex, will usually mate for life.

    When building a nest, both Chatot will find leaves, grasses, twigs, or even strips of plastic and paper and stuff them between their feathers until they return to the nesting site, where they use the materials to construct the nest itself.

    The female will lay a clutch averaging around four eggs within a week after successfully mating. Males and females will take turns incubating the eggs while the other searches for food. Within 20-25 days, the Chatot chicks hatch, blind and featherless. As with the incubation period, the parents will both take responsibility for feeding and cleaning the chicks, up until around the age of 50 days, where upon the chicks leave the nest.

    Social Structure

    Chatot are highly social Pokémon, with some flocks reaching populations of over 500 individuals. Physical affection is common amongst close individuals, with good friends and mates often perching beside and grooming one-another.

    The most dominant members, often the biggest and strongest, will assert themselves by perching high above the others, letting them know that they are capable of defending and alerting the flock of potential dangers, and thus deserve respect. While battles over dominance occur every so often, the fights rarely become serious, with the loser stepping down before either gets injured.

    Should a Chatot fall ill, it will usually be ostracized from the flock to prevent the other members from getting sick as well. A sick Chatot will often do its best to hide its condition until it recovers or becomes too ill to continue the charade. This behavior persists even in captivity, so owners are advised to immediately take their Chatot to a Pokémon Center if it shows any sign of illness, as it is usually an indicator that the Pokémon is in fact dangerously ill.

    In Human Culture

    Chatot are quite popular as pets among older children and adult due to their social nature and the novelty of their mimicry of human speech.

    Although real-life examples seem to be almost non-existent, Chatot are often depicted in media as being the companions of pirates and sailors.

    The shape of its head and crest are thought to be the inspiration behind the symbol for the musical eighth note and its derivatives.

    Trainer Tips

    Chatot love attention, and will do just about anything in order to receive it. Because of this, training them via positive reinforcement is quite easy with a supply of treats, though some Chatot are quite content with a simple head scratch and a few words of praise.

    Vital Statistics

    Born: April 22, 1985

    Biography

Thus started Misty's development as a Pokémon trainer. This was just weeks before her first day of school at Riverside Private School for Girls, an all-girls school known for its blue Picnicker-style uniforms. Among the classes she attended at Riverside were art, music, dance, and perhaps her favorite of all—swim class, which took place in a closed-off area just off of Cerulean Cape. Known to many of her peers at the time as a tomboy, she checked out all of the school's sports clubs during her first year and discovered she enjoyed basketball—a popular local sport at the time, due to Cerulean having its own International Basketball Association franchise, the Cloysters.
    Contrary to popular belief, Misty was not one of the first Gym Leaders to operate under the auspices of the Pokémon League's United Indigo Gym Leaders Association. Before her, her father Aquarius ran the local Pokémon Gym. Tragically, he was murdered by a Team Rocket grunt before her and Daisy's eyes. A few days after his funeral, his will was read, and Misty inherited not only his Starmie but also the Cerulean Gym; the Pokémon Inspection Agency confirmed that her Pokémon license was effectively in force retroactive to that date, making her the youngest League-sanctioned Gym Leader in Pokémon League history, at 9 years, 11 months, 10 days. It was around this time that the first Indigo League Elite Four gathered; one of them, a brainiac of partial Italian descent named Prima Lorelei Dellaneve, made a stop at Cerulean City on the way.
    Discovering that Misty, who was not yet 10 at the time, was already the Gym Leader, Lorelei challenged her to an exhibition match in the Gym. Misty agreed to the match, which would take place by sundown that day, and her Starmie, armed with a technique she had learned under the Squirtle Scouts, defeated Lorelei's Dewgong. Team Rocket, upon hearing about the match and its result, stayed away from her thereafter; consequently, Cerulean Gym is one of few League-sanctioned Gyms to not employ a full-time security team.
    After some time, Misty found a boyfriend in the form of a Rocker formerly employed by Vermilion Gym; one outing on Cerulean Cape ended rather unexpectedly when a trainer from Johto intruded. Displeased but wanting to see what Johto trainers were made of, Misty agreed to a Gym Battle which he proceeded to win. A couple of years later, she was among eight current and former Gym Leaders invited to take part in the annual Pokémon World Tournament in Unova; all of them still participate in each such tournament to this day, and her older sister Violet, who she had been disciplining as a Pokémon Trainer ever since her first day as a Gym Leader, operates the Gym in her absence.
