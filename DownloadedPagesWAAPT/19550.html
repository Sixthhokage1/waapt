    Subject: The Midnight Feature Urban Legend!

    In previous emails I've told you of other urban legends of the stage, screen and smaller screen and this time I'm bringing you a blast from the past with the Midnight Feature. Read on and be intriqued.

    Rumor had it, back in the day when people drove convertibles to go to their milk bars and ice-cream socials, that there was a secret reason why drive-in cinemas didn't put showings on during stormy days: That films shown during that time could see the future.

    To specify, the films wouldn't show major stuff like world events or who you'd end up dating in six months, they'd show how a random person watching would die. For this reason (and not for any practical ones), drive-in cinemas would shut their doors on rainy days to avoid people finding out about the magic in the film.

    Of course, drive-ins swiftly became a relic long before the internet got big enough for people to be bothered testing out these old legends so who knows, maybe they were telling the truth when noone serious would listen? And that, dear readers, is some cool AF lore for this week.
