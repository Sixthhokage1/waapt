    The Valkyrie 
    Route 212

    In a secluded clearing amongst the trees lining the route, a black-clad superhero is locked in combat with a young man wearing a modified Neo-Galactic uniform. His stun baton crackles in the rain as he swings at his opponent, who keeps using Detect to dodge each attack a second before it hits.

    Iapetus: No! No more defeats! This time, I will triumph!

    He swings again, only for the Avenger to duck low and jab at his stomach with a Force Palm. He crumples to the ground, groaning.

    Iapetus: Urgh...

    The Avenger takes a moment to catch their breath, then extends a hand to the loser.

    Avenger: Bad luck, Seth.

    Their defeated opponent sighs, pulls his helmet off, and heaves himself to his feet.

    Iapetus Seth: Luck had nothing to do with it. Once again, I find myself outclassed.

    The Avenger suppresses a chuckle.

    Avenger: You take this way too seriously. These are just sparring matches.

    Seth: The fact remains that I was trained by Neo-Galactic to combat those on the Auric Spectrum. I should be able to hold my own against you, with or without an Aura Jammer. Yet between your skills and your apparent immunity to the Aura Jammer's effects, you best me clearly and consistently.

    The Avenger glances down at their feet.

    Avenger: I'm sure it's just a matter of time. After all, I... it's not like I'm an expert at any of this.

    Seth: No need to be humble. I owe you a great deal.

    He bows his head.

    Seth: When I first came to you, I fully expected you to hand me back to the authorities. Instead you accepted me and gave me newfound purpose. Not since Neo-Galactic's collapse have I felt such a sense of belonging.

    The Avenger tilts their head.

    Avenger: ...You're not comparing me to Neo-Galactic, are you?

    Seth: Not at all! I... I was foolish to believe their lies. I genuinely believed that Titan offered the only path to a world free of conflict and blinded myself to any contrary evidence. Thank you for your forgiveness, and for showing me a better way.

    The Avenger rubs their arm bashfully.

    Avenger: Well... way I see it, it's not about forgiveness. I don't get to make that decision. My job is to try and help people, whatever the circumstances. You came to me looking for help, so... that's what I offered.

    There is a moment's silence.

    Avenger: ...That made sense, right?

    Seth: It did, yes.

    Avenger: Phew. I was worried that I sounded kinda crazy. Erm, that is, crazier than usual for someone who uses supernatural powers to fight crime, because, uh... that's kinda crazy to begin with. Eheheh...

    There is another awkward silence.

    Avenger: ...So, same time tomorrow?

    Seth: If you wish.

    Avenger: Great! I'll, um... I'll leave now before I say any more weird stuff.

    They abscond as quickly as possible, leaving a slightly bewildered Seth behind.
