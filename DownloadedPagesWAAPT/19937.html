    -Orlandeau walks down the hall. His business is his own, and nobody dares ask beyond that.-

    -Well, no one save his assistant.-

    Orlandeau: You've been at my shoulder for twenty yards, Miss Prokleti.

    -The pale girl, her ear-length hair frizzed up in every direction, looks as if she hadn't gotten sleep in the last week.-

    Miss Prokleti: I've been trying to get your attention, sir.

    -Her voice, even when conversing, barely goes over a whisper. In fact, if he hadn't been able to predict what she was saying, Dr. Orlandeau would have needed to ask her to repeat herself.-

    Orlandeau: Then tap my shoulder. What is it?

    -He turns around, his tall, lanky frame dwarfing the barely five-and-a-half foot woman. Her goggles shake as she stops on a dime, and she takes a moment to fix them.-

    Miss Prokleti: I've located several possible locations for off-ship bases for our department's needs. All have a remarkable variety in local ecology and possible test subjects.

    -She hands him a clipboard, a stack of papers on it. Flipping through the pages, Orlandeau's eyes remain cold.-

    Orlandeau: Marsh, excellent ecological variety but horribly expensive to create permanent housing and infrastructure... Coral reef, too often patrolled by conservation personnel...

    -Miss Prokleti taps her fingers together, her shoulders shaking. Orlandeau is about ready to pass the clipboard back before something catches his eye.-

    Orlandeau: ... "Abandoned geothermal research station"?

    -Prokleti jumps, shaking her goggles loose.-

    Miss Prokleti: W-w-w-well, sir, the station is on a minor island of the Alola island chain, not often visited by people or authorities, and isn't being watched by any of Alola's famous "Guardians". There's also a few local Pokemon with fascinating interactions regarding venom, poison, and the purification thereof.

    -Orlandeau looks at it with a little more detail. Abandoned for about twenty years... no regular visitors... and holy crap look at that the entire facility is quake-proofed.-

    Orlandeau: ... I will inspect this area personally.

    -He takes the paper and hands the clipboard back to his assistant.-

    Orlandeau: Good work, Miss Prokleti.

    -The little frazzle-haired woman nods enthusiastically as she takes the clipboard and slinks off. Orlandeau blinks - for being such a small, jittery young woman, she moves surprisingly quietly.-

    -Aulstyne shrugs.-

    Aulstyne: I vill admit, hy did not expect hyu to have such a good answer.

    -She pushes off of the wall and raps the brim of her hat.-

    Aulstyne: Hy am not so egotistical zat I vill push to go on some minor recon mission. Und hy know about your past vith Doktor Bunansa.

    -Aulstyne shows remarkable remorse at that last remark. Orlandeau blinks and makes a slight reconsideration to the usually sociopathic Germanic.-

    Aulstyne: However. Vhen hyu finish vith your project, ve vill need to discuss accommodations.

    -Orlandeau tilts his head.-

    Aulstyne: Hyu are ze only administrator to really take initiative regarding your plans. Doktor Bunansa vants me to make sure hyu are not... interrupted.

    -Orlandeau's brow furrows.-

    Orlandeau: You want to play nanny?

    -Aulstyne spits on the floor, a darkness shadowing her face.-

    Aulstyne: I'd rather have sex vith ze business end of a lawnmower. But success for hyu means success for me. My department's objectives are "Security, Combat, und Acquisition", und zwei of zose drei require infrastructure.

    -Orlandeau waves a hand at the pink-haired woman.-

    Orlandeau: And you haven't started planning for your own base, because...?

    Aulstyne: Money. As necessary as my department is, hy can't guarantee zat ve vill produce enough resources to support further growth.

    Orlandeau: So you want us - or right now, just me - to grow the organization enough to where you can become independent?

    Aulstyne: Yes. Now...

    -Her face grows dark, but her eyes glow a devilish red.-
