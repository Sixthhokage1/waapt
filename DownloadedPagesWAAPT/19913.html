    Illusion HQ Ruins, a while back

    (It was the closest to peaceful hed been in how long? He had lost track. The moment Cassie disappeared, any urge to keep track of time had evaporated.)

    (For all he may have lost, for all his mind had been slowly deteriorating, Adam still feared death. That he worshipped its living embodiment simply made such fear more palpable, in a way - hed always viewed his own inevitable demise as the final sacrifice he was to make in his Ladys name, giving himself entirely so she may continue to do her work. But now he was dying, he could tell, and it did not feel anything like a sacrifice or an act of torture. He simply felt tired - tired and peaceful and happy? Well. He hadnt been happy since the day Reichardt had betrayed him so sadistically. But now there was a warm, dark, yet comforting blanket enveloping him, and even he was finding it hard to muster up any negative thoughts. Only peaceful acceptance remained.)

    (...)

    (The floor was gone. There was no more sensation of cold metal beneath him, nor any sensation at all. He felt as if he were floating in empty space.)

    ???: <Oh, hello, Adam.>

    (A familiar winged figure swooped into view, but its voice was distinctly male.)

    Adam: <...my Lad->

    (He stops himself. That wasnt right)

    Adam: <Sorry, my Lord? Is it truly you?>

    Yvan: -scowls- <Of course it is I! I am both male and female! But that's not possible from your skewed perspective, is it?!>

    (There is a pause. The darkness is so quiet, you can almost hear something deep in the recesses of Adam's brain screech in agony. Eventually, he decides to avoid openly confronting the issue. Maybe maybe his Lord hadn't meant what he thought he meant.)

    Adam: <So is this it? Is this the end of my life?>

    Yvan: <Not quite, and you're lucky it isn't... Your transphobia is one of many -phobias and -isms and whatnot that number among this list of rather nasty things would send you to a rather nasty part of the Spirit World...>

    (he produces a scroll he unfurls that says "ADAM'S SINS" at the top and reveals an absurdly long list that rolls onward and onward into the void. Adam cannot see all of it, but he sees enough of it to get the point across.)

    Adam: <...>

    (His eyes narrow viciously.)

    <No.>

    Yvan: <Yes! You are a toxic, awful being, Adam, and you are lucky this is only a fleeting early visit to my doorstep - and in your case I can afford to change my mind.>

    Adam: <   ...   >

    (His fully opened eye suddenly rolls dangerously to one side, accompanied by a harsh, unhinged cackle.)

    <And and to think I believed for a second you were truly Yveltal, praise be to her! How foolish I was!>

    (His eye refocuses to glare at Yvan.)

    Adam: <Now listen to me, you vile blasphemer of Her Holiness's sacred image! I have never sinned - never! My entire life has been a quest to cleanse this world of its sins and transgressions, no matter how much it may have pained me! All in your - no, her - name! My Lady would never treat her most loyal retainer in this way! My lady would never - never - be willing to transform herself into a vile, degenerate fa->

    (Adam is cut off by being blasted by an Oblivion Wing right there.)

    Yvan: <Whoopsie! That's a few years off your lifespan already, blasphemer - delicious! You talk of me being false, but you are no true retainer of mine!>

    (The Oblivion Wing sends Adam rotating into the inky blackness. He hangs there for what seems like an eternity, struggling to process what is happening.)

    Adam: <N-no this can't be true, I can't have been lying my whole life I am enlightened, I am light and cleanliness in a dark, filthy world...>

    Yvan: -chuckles bitterly- <More like a stain that's particularly rotten and gross.>

    (Adam stares at Yveltal, a mania starting to build in his mismatched eyes.)

    Adam: <I I opposed those who would sully your glorious name! I directed my righteous fury at the evils spawned by the false gods! How can you call such devotion sin?>

    Yveltal: <For all you have wounded, physically and mentally, in the process! How could I forgive you for delivering such pain! How could I forgive you for that?! How could you forgive yourself!?>

    Adam: <They deserved it! Do you really think I would have exiled my six children - my six beautiful, misled children - if I had any choice? I must protect your name from the blasphemies of Arceus - of Xerneas - of all those false gods who imitate your almighty power!>

    Yvan: <Newsflash, they're real! Xerneas is my beloved brother! You've done nothing but further blasphemy and pain!>

    (Adam stares for a good, long minute. His eyes slide ever further out of sync.)

    Adam: (softly) <You can't be real I refuse to let you be real>

    Yvan: <Oh, but I am real... As is this vision of what hell is like for mons like you!>

    (with a dramatic wave of his wing Yvan summons a silver screen behind himself...)

    (...that promply plays the following music video:)

    (Yvan just stares silently in shock at the screen the entire time. He maintains barely enough self-control to keep his scream of anger internal.)

    Yvan: LEVIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIIII-

    (Adam is staring at the screen thoughout the video, his look of incredulity slowly morphing into one of completely genuine horror.)

    Adam: <No I understand what this means>

    (Upon hearing Adam's words Yvan's shocked expression turns into a malicious grin of the sort decidedly appropriate for the season this is being posted◊.)

    Yvan: <...Go on?>

    Adam: <At first, I believed this simply to be a prank, nothing but the worthless babblings of a group of degenerates but then I realised this was the point. To be trapped amongst hordes of the unwashed and unsaved, forced to watch as they debase themselves>

    (He shudders.)

    Adam: <What could be worse for a true believer?

    (Yvan cackles)

    Yvan: <Nothing! And that is why you must change your ways, Adam - to be spared of an eternity with everything you hate!>

    (Adam is barely paying attention. His eyes are completely glossed over and out of sync, his head tilted into his paws as he babbles to himself in a terrified whimper.)

    Adam: <Truly, they must be Yveltal to know so well what I fear, and to disguise it so nonchalantly as mere camp! But if that is the case>

    (He looks back at Yveltal with a completely unreadable, deranged expression.)

    Adam: <Maybe maybe there was never a truth never any enlightenment to be had I've wasted my entire life in pursuit of a worthless fantasy>

    (The mania in his eyes reaches a fever pitch as his voice transforms into a barely coherent babble.)

    Adam: <B-but if I am worth nothing then what worth is anything? At all?>

    Yvan: <You must find out for yourself what is of true worth in life, Adam... I came to warn, not give you answers.>

    (Adam lets out a strange, high-pitched giggle as his head lolls dangerously to one side.)

    Adam: <M-my whole life was devoted to letting you lead me, my Lord! Without you I am nothing no, this world is nothing!>

    (Another laugh - this time, a second disturbing, harsh cackle.)

    Adam: <I've seen the filth in this world ever since I was a child, you know! When I found Yvetalism as an adult, it gave me a purpose - no longer was I a delinquent lashing out for no reason! I was a prophet! A bringer of -heh- good news! What worth am I without a cause?>

    Yvan: <I'll give you a hint: Try being nice for a change?>

    Adam: <NO!>

    (He snarls.)

    Adam: <This cruel, diseased universe deserves no love! And if spirituality cannot help me cure it, I-I'll just have to keep searching until I find the true cure!>

    (Yvan scoffs.)

    Yvan: <I literally just gave you a damn good lead you oh-so flippantly denied.>

    (Something was tugging at the back of Adam's head, pulling him away. He barely even noticed, however, so intense was his now-rootless fury and madness.)

    Adam: <Stop it! Stop it! I want a cure! P-please, just give me the cure! I just want to save this world, can't you see? What good will it do to treat those around me as if there's nothing wrong with them? Th-they're diseased! I can tell! I've always been able to tell!>

    Yvan: <No, really, give the whole kindness thing a try. It may just be the cure you need.>

    (Adam's eyes were burning. His skull throbbed. Suddenly he could barely think, barely even see Yveltal for the bright light consuming his brain.)

    Adam: <N-no I don't want to be a liar>

    Yvan: <Pants on fire.>

    (he screeches and lunges at Adam with his talons. Adam instinctively flinches, and in that moment his entire body is forcibly wrenched away; he hurtles through the empty space, which has gone from pitch black to blindingly bright; the pain is absurd, it feels as if his skull is about to explode-)

    Neo-Illusion HQ, between then and now

    (...and just as suddenly, the pain is gone. He feels nothing but a broken lethargy as he stares up at a barely-visible humanoid shape. An unfamiliar voice echoes distantly in his ears.)

    ???: Heh. Rise and shine, Adam.

    ???

    (Yvan twitches)

    <...I owe Levi nothing.>

    (He flies off into the inky blackness.)
