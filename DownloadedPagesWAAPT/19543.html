    Celestic Town

    Having recovered from his brief bout of insanity-induced catatonia, Shun has now returned to his hometown, though his expression suggests that he's hardly overjoyed about it. Gregson is following behind him.

    Gregson: <...Sure feels weird to be back here.>

    Shun says nothing. He keeps his head low and his hat pulled down until he comes to a small bungalow and knocks on the door. After a moment, the door is opened by an old man of at least eighty, whose face breaks into a wide smile as he begins to speak in a thick country accent.

    Shun's grandfather: Shun! Yer a sight for sore eyes, I'll tell ya that.

    He pulls Shun into a hug, which Shun returns somewhat awkwardly.

    Shun: Hello again, Grandpa.

    Shun's grandfather: 'Ello? You've been gone for two years, and that's all ya can think ta say?

    Shun: I, erm...

    Shun's grandfather laughs.

    Shun's grandfather: I'm only knockin' ya. Almost forgot how easy it was to make ya light up like that! Come in. The sun's gonna boil ya in that suit of yours.

    He leads Shun and Gregson into the sitting room, then sticks his head round a door and starts yelling.

    Shun's grandfather: Ken! Get yerself over here!

    Kenji: Urgh, do I have to? I'm in the middle of a match!

    Shun's grandfather: 'Fraid so! Yer brother's here!

    A boy in a wheelchair, who looks to be about two or three years older than Shun, manuevres himself into the sitting room.

    Kenji: There goes my streak.

    Shun: Kenji. Good to see you again.

    Kenji: ...Yeah. Same.

    Shun's grandfather: I'll go get some drinks. You boys play nice now.

    He heads into the kitchen, leaving Shun and Kenji sitting together in silence. Kenji breaks it first.

    Kenji: ...Solve any crimes lately?

    Shun: My job is not quite as simple as you make it sound.

    Kenji: So no, then.

    He shrugs.

    Kenji: You could start by busting those robbers over in Hearthome. I saw them on the news. They looked pretty cool.

    Shun: I saw the reports, and no, they were most certainly not "cool". Lives and property were being put at risk. Somebody could have been seriously hurt as a result of such reckless and irresponsible behaviour.

    Kenji snorts.

    Kenji: Alright, calm down, grandpa. I was only saying.

    Shun: Then perhaps you ought to refrain from saying in future.

    Shun's grandfather sticks his head through the door.

    Shun's grandfather: Somebody call me?

    Shun: No, we were just-

    Shun's grandfather: Well, that's a cryin' shame. My poor ol' bladder won't be able to cope with all this tea all by itself.

    Kenji: I'll pass, thanks. I'm not going to get any better at maining Reaper by sitting here drinking tea.

    He retreats back into his room, leaving Shun with his grandfather and a tray of tea.

    Shun's grandfather: Still gettin' along like two Furrets in a sack, eh?

    Shun: He's the one who insists on being difficult.

    Shun's grandfather: I dunno, I'd say both of ya can be pretty difficult at times. I should know, I'm the one who had to stop you from killin' each other.

    He chuckles, then pours himself and Shun a cup of green tea.

    Shun's grandfather: Anyways, how have ya been? I never hear from ya. Would it kill ya to call yer poor ol' granddad once in a while, huh?

    Shun: I'm sorry. I've been preoccupied with-

    Shun's grandfather: Ah, I'm jus' messin' with ya. I know yer busy. It was the same back in my day. Don't gemme wrong, I loved the job, but I wouldn't go back to it. I'm past it. I'll jus' sit here and waste away whilst you young'uns do all the legwork.

    He smiles.

    Shun's grandfather: I ain't lettin' you outta here till you tell me what you've been up to, though.

    Shun: Very well.
