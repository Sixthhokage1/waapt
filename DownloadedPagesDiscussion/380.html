    Legendary issue

    Thinking about it a bit more, I'd say Ho-oh is actually not that good of an example anymore. Sure, he's appeared the most out of any Legendary, but that's from a general view. In the past 8 months, he has only appeared once, and even then he appeared at his home, hidden. Nix has also only appeared once, in a festival specifically dedicated to her. And each appearance was fairly brief. Meanwhile, a few other Legendaries have had either more or longer appearances.

    The illusion of Ho-oh appearing more often is created by the fact that he's talked about more often. And this happens both in-RP and in the games, so it's not necessarily our fault. I'm not saying we shouldn't worry about it, but we shouldn't make such a big deal about that.

    However, we should make sure to keep "casual visits" to Legendaries to a minimum. They're not always gonna be at home, even if they are disguised as Pidgey to avoid people making a big deal out of it. I'm not saying we're having a big problem with it at the moment, but we shouldn't create the problem merely to have a reason to fix it.

    And I probably could've worded that last sentence much better, but you get the point.
    Key word "mostly".

    Umbra's Conquest Characters

    Because might as well outline it properly somewhere.

    Ghost Lord Umbramatic

    Name: Umbramatic, though usually referred to as "The Ghost Lord"

    Real Name: Joseph Nox

    Gender: Male

    Occupation: Evil Overlord

    Region: Spectra

    Yes, this is the same Umbra you see in the modern day... Except thanks to using the high-priest privileges he got over the years he's worked his way up Spectra's political ladder to become Warlord and rule it with an iron fist. He's got minions galore - mostly Ghosts, but he's no one-type pony, and has plenty of other cronies, magically-mutated and otherwise. He's no slouch fighting himself - his sword, Shadow Force, and his enhanced Obscura powers make him a formidable foe. Be warned- the one person he doesn't have a ruthless attitude towards is his wife, Duolis. (Not sure if Pent is writing for her or not.)

    Umbra's Minions

    Ghost Army: Various Ghosts, all willing to serve Umbra's very whim, so long as they get a taste of DAT LIFE FORCE. Many are assigned to Umbra's human minions, others are assigned to possess the mutants.

    "Demons": A few mooks of Umbra's grow daring, and ask for more power. Umbra gives it to them in the form of mutations, which turn them into monstrous creatures that serve him in taking down threats to his rule. Recently, most of them have been tasked with thwarting a certain White Arcanine.

    Lumos: Male Chandelure. Normally the voice of reason of the team, and the one who keeps Umbra in line... Except he's too smitten with Bow to pay attention to his Trainer's rise to power and resulting atrocities.

    Mata Nui: Golurk. Umbra's personal bodyguard. Thinks Umbra is an awesome, and wouldn't do anything wrong! Right?

    Styx: Female Sableye. Secretly Umbra's back-from-the-dead sister, but afraid to tell him as he falls further and further into darkness.

    Bow: Female Dusknoir. Motherly, but loyal to Umbra. Often possesses him and others for various reasons.

    Spider Queen: Female Mutant Ariados. One of the Demon Generals, but the lowest-ranking one. She likes lurking in dungeons and eating an travelers or adventurers that dare come across her.

    Crimson Helmet: Male Mutant Tauros. Dumb brute, but that doesn't stop him from being one of the Demon Generals. Likes smashing things, setting them on fire, or both.

    Blight: Male Mutant Gastly. May not seem like much due to his diminutive size, but he can invade the bodies of others and wreak havok, earning him a spot as a Demon General.

    Ninetails: Female Mutant Ninetales. Cunning as well as powerful, she fights alongside her Smeargle mate to infuriate her foes. (Read: Counter-brush techniques.) When she's not crushing others in battle, she's using her illusions to deceive, or disciplining her daughter Rosa.

    Orochi: Male Mutant Hydreigon. This eight headed monstrosity has a fondness for terrorizing villages and maiden sacrifices. He has a strong taste for sake, however, which can be easily exploited.

    Lechku and Nechku: Mechanical Noctowl. Not technically Pokemon, but clockwork constructs. Don't let their dapper looks fool you - these mechanical not-birds are quite formidable in combat.

    Yami: Unknown Leaning towards Finneon or Seel. This mechanical terror is shrouded in mystery, with many legends about it being a lord of darkness that would blot out the sun. Some say it pilfered its technology from an ancient civilization, but whatever the case, it is certainly something to be feared.

    Crimson Lady Lina

    Name: Lina, though usually referred to as the Crimson Lady.

    Real Name: Circe Titania

    Gender: Female

    Occupation: Warlord

    Region: Dragnor

    Like Umbra, Lina has made it to high places in Ransei. Unlike Umbra, she rules more fairly... But that doesn't stop her from being a powerful warrior alongside her husband Kaius. She sees the mysterious Ghost Lord as a threat to her region and all of Ransei, and wants to stop him at all costs... Even if she's never seen his face. She wields a sword named Spiritslayer, and with her bonded Pokemon and Obscura skills she's a force to be reckoned with.

    Lina's Mons

    Chrome: Metagross. Lina's oldest team member, and generally the wisest. Calculating and intelligent, he's Lina's go-to strategist for battles.

    Ultra: Male Bisharp. The other voice of reason on the team, he's usually exasperated by non-Chrome members, but gets by.

    Filia: Female Dragonite. Rough, tough, and not afraid to utterly smash opponents when asked -or not asked. Has a softer side, but is a bit afraid to show it.

    Cera: Female Aggron. Don't be fooled by her looks- Cera has the personality associated with a much, smaller, cuter, more effeminate mon. (Think Fortis in an Aggron's body.) To emphasis this, her main battling strategy is to hug her opponents - which, given her size and strength, is actually strangely effective.

    Vito: Male Honchkrow. This guy is a stereotypical mobster, with a bad accent and butchered Gratuitous Italian to boot. He fights REALLY dirty, and can even function as an assassin when need be.

    Sun Goddess Amaterasu

    Name: Amaterasu, though usually referred to as the White Arcanine or Ammy.

    Real Name: Amaterasu Crescendo

    Gender: Female

    Occupation: Force of Good

    Region: N/A

    Ammy, unlike the others, isn't in this time naturally. Punted to the past by a reality-warping bork, she's now neither human nor Volcarona, but a White Arcanine with blazing red stripes. Upon reaching the past, she's sucked into a conspiracy that branches over all of Ransei... But with Arcanine's moves, a plethora of weapons, a mysterious paintbrush, and many helpers and tools on her journey, she stands more than just a chance.

    Ammy's Allies

    Issun: Small, spunky, and always willing to give [tips], Issun is an important guide on Ammy's journey. He's taken up painting recently, but it remains to be seen whether that will go anywhere.

    Susano'o: Not to be confused with his nerdy descendant, this Samurott is bold, brash, and downright dumb. He has a heroic streak in there somewhere, though, and Ammy can help unlock it.

    Waka: This familiar, Latios-themed man seems flamboyant, but that conceals a ferocious skill in battle. It is unclear whether he wants to help or thwart Ammy, but it is said he has a grudge against some of her enemies himself.

    Sakuya: This cherry blossom spirit is important in Ammy's early journey, as her Guardian Saplings must be restored to reverse a curse on the land.

    The Brush Gods: These twelve spirits appear at points in Ammy's journey to assist her, and grant her new abilities called "Brush Techniques." Personality-wise, they all may seem... Familiar.

    Shiranui: Who is this other White Arcanine, said to have taken down Orochi the first time? Whatever the answer, he exudes a power and grace that puts fear in foe's hearts.

    Various Others: Ammy has a large and colorful cast assisting her on her journey. There's far too many to list here, but they all play a key role of some sort.
