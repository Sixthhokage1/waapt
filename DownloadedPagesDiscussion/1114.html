    27850 Umbramatic20th Feb 2016 10:12:42 PM from WAAPT or PEFE Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: In another castle
    The eternal dusk returns

    tl;dr that escalated quickly◊

    So... This is that explanation/clarification of what led me to get Pippi and I hunkered down for a whole month to eventually punt out the rest of the Ballad Of Robin yesterday with the assistance of Bitters, Crow, and Tagg.

    As a reminder, after the initial bits of the Delta Episode had been completed, I focused on and successfully completed two other extremely important parts of my endgame: HTYD (Concluding Nico's character arc) and Werehuman Madness (likewise concluding the Jerkbros' character arcs). On the side though, me and Pippi were getting into the Meteor Falls segment of the DE and decided it was a good idea for purposes of the plot if Vyvyan and Robin did some life-story exchanging as a prelude to the overarching plot stuff. On Vyvyan's end it was pretty straightforward, since his story had already been depicted in-RP (and Pippi said she admittedly remembered less of what went down in Hoenn-1 than I did), but come Robin's turn things got more complicated since aside from a few minor things I'd kept details on what went down in his essential home turf - Fire Emblem Awakening's Bad End timeline - about as ambiguous as in Awakening itself. From there we decided that "eh, we're not likely to ever get a canon answer there, let's just make up what happened ourselves." But from there it got a fair bit more elaborate, as we had to use what little details we did know about the Bad End timeline to build our own version, and with so many empty gaps we had to coordinate like crazy to fill them, and it wasn't long before we had Fire Emblem Awakening: The Abridged Series on our hands.

    But the real killer was all the godawful delays holding everything back; on my end first was finishing the other subplots mentioned above, but when those finished and things went breifly more smoothly they were promptly derailed by holiday and vacation shenanigans on my end and then plenty of unexpected stuff after - and not only did this frustrate both Pippi and immensely getting the Ballad of Robin out, but Pippi eventually informed me it was frustrating others as well, and she was really worried because of it.

    Thing is though, up and quitting at that point was no longer an option, for multiple reasons:

    • Me and Pippi had still made a good amount of progress at that point, and we were really liking how things were turning out even then.

    • It actually ended up pretty much being really frigging important - all that stuff about Robin's backstory expanded a lot on both his circumstances and character in a really nice way and much of it wound up really conveniently tying into later parts of the Delta Episode and even beyond.

    • And tied to the above, people were actually enjoying the Ballad of Robin itself; a lot of players genuinely really liked the expansion of Robin's backstory and worldbuilding for the Bad End timeline, and the people complaining were more just as frustrated with the delays and how they were holding up both the Ballad of Robin and the already-horribly-0delayed Delta Episode as we were.

    So in light of all that, and knowing TSing-within-TSing would be suicidal for multiple reasons, Pippi and I decided to keep quiet (and I decided to go dark as possible contacts-wise) to hunker down and spag down the remainder in one go. That proved an immense ordeal in and of itself - I still had plenty of nasty RL bullshit to deal with (especially last couple weeks of January) me and Pippi both had to deal with horrible exhaustion and headaches from both med changes and staying up godawfully late to work together on the thing, and even after I brought in multiple other people to help with planning and coordinating to ease the load on Pippi's end there I eventually had to deal with a new and decidedly uncooperative Internet connection.

    ...But eventually, with the help of the others, Pippi and I finally got the thing done◊. The spagdoc was 111 pages and nearly 25,000 words long - enough to count as a novella even without the previous instalments. And after everybody put it through some fine-tuning we had time to be trolly and drop it the day of Fire Emblem Fates' release.

    And while I would be lying if I said we all weren't immensely relieved that whole ordeal was over - I'd also be lying if I said we weren't damn well satisfied with how the fruits of our labors turned out.

    ...But regardless, I need to say this: I'm really frigging sorry we inadvertently derailed the Delta Episode with a Fire Emblem fic, really frigging appreciate that a lot of you still enjoyed said fic regardless, and will get back to the Delta Episode proper first thing next week after I use this weekend to take it easy - because man, I'm exhausted.

    (On a more positive note, I've got some fun announcements and goodies planned in the wake of the Ballad of Robin's completion, so stay tuned!)
