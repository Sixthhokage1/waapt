    Kay. In the meantime, Discussion's been getting a lot more use, so discuss things here and such.

    And on another note, since it's been about a year since the last one, it's time for the 2015 Mon Census!

    What's the Mon Census?

    It's census of the Pokemon that have appeared in the RP up to now or in the near future.

    What do I do for it?

    Follow this link and add any Pokemon you feel are significant to be added in the Census. It's greatly encouraged that everyone do this in order to give us the best data.

    Also, please only add Pokemon you write for.

    What do you mean by significant?

    Mons that fit any of these criteria:

    • They appear in an average or more than average percentage of your posts.
    • They are/were integral to any arc, plot, or subplot since your entry into the RP.
    • They are planned to be or are currently part of some sort of event/appearance in the RP.
    • They are important enough to your character that they warrant notice and representation of the species as a whole.

    Generally, I would say J-Team Mons would qualify for at least one of these unless their player is never planning on writing for them again, but I'll leave that decision up to the player who enters them. This criteria is more to keep people from adding, say, Pokemon they included in one post and are never going to have appear again than keeping people from adding characters they don't feel they've written enough for recently. Make sure to list the Pokemon's name as well as their species in brackets, such as 'M. Bush [Venusaur]' unless their species is their name or there's only one available entry in the column.

    Why are there separate entries for Pokemon like Oddish and Eevee?

    We're cataloging split evolutions separately. We also listed Eevee on its own for Eevee who aren't close to evolving or don't know what evolution they'll be yet. For other Pokemon with branched evolutions, just make your best guess.

    Why does it say 'OU'?

    This census is OU-specific for three reasons:

    • There was more going on depicted in alternate universes when the census was first created
    • The depiction of the Pokemon population of said alternate universes has not significantly changed to my knowledge
    • Some Pokemon evolved differently/didn't evolve in other universes and it might cause some difficulties to tally them all in one sheet.

    If interest is strong enough, I can open a different tally for a desired alternate universe or merge them if we figure out how to best do so.

    What about Pokeforms?

    As a general rule, do not count Pokeforms. However, if a character would be counted as a member of their Pokeform's species by themselves and those who know them well enough, then I would say they should be counted.

    I'm planning on introducing a Pokemon character in the near future. Should I include them?

    If it's within a few months, you may include them if you so desire.

    Where/When will these results be tallied?

    In this wiki page when it doesn't seem like there are going to be any more additions.

    Now, with that cleared up, feel free to start adding!

    And if there's any contention on any of these points, feel free to bring it up
