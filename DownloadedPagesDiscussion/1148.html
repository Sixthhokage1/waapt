    I didn't say anything previously because I figured that was a decision for the "plot makers" or long time members and not someone like me. But (especially due to recent events), I've actually got some thoughts now.

    My major concern when it comes to being a Frontier Brain (and this applies to basically any sort of title, except maybe being a hall-of-famer or having [X] number of badges) is the possibility of turning inter-player battles into a contests of people whipping out their character's resumes to determine who is going to win a battle rather than encouraging a habit of the players/authors discussing what result(s) would be most interesting for both of their characters or just agreeing to RP it out and see what happens, Frankly, I've already seen "my accomplishments mean you can't win" scenarios happen a few times in the RP (fortunately it's been rare).
    And I might be a little guilty of it as well when it comes to battles involving pokeforms, If so, I'm working on that

    From what I understand this is supposed to be a RP where even a little Sandshrew can win against a fully evolved mega mon if it's clever, lucky and (maybe) cute enough. We don't determine battles by "levels" and "EXP", likewise experiences/accomplishments should be a factor, but not the be-all and end-all of inter-player interactions.

    Now with that mini-rant over... Thoughts on Sarah actually becoming a Frontier Brain:

    I don't know where I stand on that yet. The fact she's not replacing any canon NPC's positions helps... But I'm VERY murky on how the Brains operate in the first place, and I'm not quite sure if I'm comfortable with, say, Sarah getting her own Battle [X] facility in the frontier and her own custom badge/emblem/(I forget what they're called).

    On the other hand, if anyone were to ever challenge the Hoenn Frontier, the possibility of having Sarah be a replacement for one of the battle does sound potentially interesting. But from what I can tell she can kind of already do that?

    I guess for the time being I'd like a bit more information on how Frontier-Brain!Sarah would theoretically affect the rest of the Frontier.

    "My accomplishments mean you can't win"

    Understandable, I've admittedly been subject to this issue before, but I do want to make a point to show that the Battle Frontier isn't supposed to operate in terms of levels even in-game — they're entirely based on skill, considering one mode limits the max level to 50 and the other adjusts your opponents to be no stronger than your own highest level Pokemon. I'm gonna try my best to have Sarah's abilities reflect this. 

    Battle Facilities

    At first, she wouldn't even have a Battle Facility of her own; even beyond coming up with a gimmick, buildings like these take a lot of time and a lot of resources. Instead, at least at first, I was considering having Sarah become a part-time co-Brain of the Battle Palace, and the rest of the time she'd be a "wandering" Frontier Brain — instead of having a gauntlet of trainers to fight, challengers would have to seek her out and face her wherever she happens to be, no matter the location. I think it would work out well with her being a J-Teamer. 

    Nepotism, ho!

    Ellen's existence is admittedly something I hadn't considered, although Sarah's whole arc has been more or less based on her making a name for herself despite, rather than because of, her mother's status as a former Champion. And Ellen actually doesn't do much in regards to the current League anyway — partly because she likes keeping the "how in the world is she back we have her official death record right here" thing going, and having to deal with League officials other than keeping secrets with the Gym Leaders, E4, and Champions could easily put a damper on that.
