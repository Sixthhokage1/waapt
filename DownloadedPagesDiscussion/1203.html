    Tickets to Confusion

    Also with the Incursionplot over, I felt I'd establish a bit more on how the Tickets to Unova work, since there's a lot of misassumptions about how they function and I was always intending to lay some ground rules for the WAAP Tverse officially having cosmic keystones (including making sure they are VERY VERY VERY restricted and moderated in use). 

     The Lowdown

    The Tickets To Unova are the metaphysical keystones of the OU and any universes diverged directly from it. As such, the common presumption that they needed to be thrown in a volcano to create a brand-new universe is false - it's closer to how that bit of Homestuck that particular sequence is referencing works, yes, but if you read the post closely the Tickets aren't actually destroyed in said sequence, since they can't be destroyed that easily, and if they were destroyed it'd wipe a good chunk of the multiverse since the Tickets, again, are cosmic keystones of the OU and related universes.

    They were probably granted that power retroactively by Arceus after the defeat of Missingno or M, or possibly had it all along. Regardless, they're now intrinsically bound to the more WAAPT-y parts of the multiverse and can travel beyond the bounds of spacetime within them as I attempted to crudely illustrate with this chart◊. The stuff marked off by a checkpoint is somewhere the Tickets can move along the bounds of spacetime freely; the stuff marked with Xes are examples of places where they cannot. The Yangverse is an odd case since even though it was born from the OU it's it's own universe spacetime/metaphysics-wise and so the Tickets cannot go there.

    Wherever they show up on the spacetime continuum, though, the Tickets are extraordinarily powerful reality-warping objects, but do not hand out their power lightly, and can only pull utterly cosmic feats for others with a sufficient extra power source - like a planet full of Glitch energy. Again, any use of these things in plots will have to be strictly moderated - though I feel it may be appropriate to invoke them in the Re: Glitch climax somehow.

    Finally, while Team Umbra is shown using six and their number was obviously once finite, there is now a perpetual Schrodinger's number of them due to their cosmic nature.

    Please, do bring up any questions or concerns. But thirdly...
