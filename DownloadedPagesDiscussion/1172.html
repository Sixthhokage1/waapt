    Alright, folks, HAVE A REINTRODUCTION TO MY MAIN CHARACTERS ALL UP IN YOUR GRILL

    (Note these guys are the results of four and a half years of expanding and revising their characters, so newbies, do not try this at home yet.)

    Team Umbra (AKA my P Cs)

    Yep, these are the main characters of my WAAPT stories, (depicted in order of debut here◊ by my talented cousin Martha) and they all have very weird and wild individual stories of their own. In order of debut:

    Umbra: My eponymous first PC◊, back when eponymous first P Cs were the norm. Rendered functionally immortal with Obscura powers by a Giratinist cult, he used that to his advantage to go full evil overlord as the Ghost Lord◊, complete with a one-winged angel form known as Penumbra◊... Only to be taken down by his time-traveling girlfriend from the future who I'll get to in a bit. After realizing he'd been a douche he shut himself away for 600 years before he emerged to find a new life and atone... Only to discover many new things about his life, those he affected in his past (including several literal ghosts he has varying relationships with, and one descendant by the name of our own Pent) and more upon being roped into the J-Team. Personality-wise is usually laid-back, caring, and friendly, but also easily exasperated, has notably petty tendencies (including a fittingly immortal-tier tendency to get grudgey), and is not above trying to cause trouble if he thinks he has a justification.

    Lina: Second member of Team Umbra◊ notably fond of red and generally unfond of ghosts; a backup in the same experiment that created Umbra, she fittingly was his rival he never met back when he ruled as the Ghost Lord, herself a more benevolent ruler known as the Crimson Lady◊ that inaverdaently invented the four-move limit in official Pokemon battles. Since then, she's wandered the world and shaped history many times over, and when she finally met Umbra face-to face, she got to know him as he'd changed enough to be shocked when learning he was the dreaded Ghost Lord. Despite this and other difficulties in their relationship, they're now good friends, and Lina serves as a guide and mentor to both her fellow Team Umbra members as well as her own descendants, the Amarins. Generally street-smart, stern and no-nonsense, she nonetheless has both a soft side and a fiery temper; on the latter, do not mention her diminutive 5'0 stature visibly or audibly if you want to live.

    Ammy: third member of Team Umbra◊, who is actually a Volcarona from the PMD-B timeline where an alternate Alduin the Hydreigon and his forces successfully polymorphed enough of humanity into Pokemon to render them a critically endangered species; she herself is the daughter of an alternate, monifuied version of Mezzo's character Jenna and grandaughter through her Flygon father's side of alternate versions of Ever's Flygon Marmalade and Speck, who belonged to an NPC of former player Tracer. After working with Umbra to free herself from a Mother Gothel-esqe Haxorus stepmother and meeting with her biological parents, she managed to get ahold of a Pokemon -> human armband, and through abuse of it, become a trainer specializing in Bug types when she came to the main RP timeline. After that and other complications, she entered a romantic relationship with Umbra... Which theen had his own complications, including her having to go back in time as a divine Arcanine◊ to kick his Ghost Lord ass. They've managed to stay together and have a happy relationship overall, though, and are due to get married in the near future; Ammy also has close friendships with the rest of Team Umbra and others in the J-Team. She's very excitable, bubbly in a good mood. and energetic, but knows when to get serious if need be; however she's also rather easily discouraged at times and highly impulsive in general, the latter in particular when it comes to her love of    burning.   

    Arika:, depicted on the right with a PC coming up on the left◊, is a Water monotyper whose mood is as wild and varied as the ocean - she can be anything from a giddy nerd to calm and collected to highly exasperated or even psychotic depending on the situaion. This is because the Arika we know has a wild and convoluted origin story - while effectively Native Alohan in looks, ethnicity, and adoptive heritage, she was once a powerful wish-granting artifact called the Lunar Pendant that abused a cosmic loophole to grant its own wish to be human after being lost for hundreds of years. During the days of Umbra and Lina's reigns as the Ghost Lord and Crimson Lady in Ransei, the Lunar Pendant absorbed the soul of an assassin named Ren Kaiken◊; initially, the Pendant's existing personality and Ren's inaverdeantly suppresed each other as Arika, but after Arika learned of and initially struggled with her true nature, they underwent a full-on Split-Personality Merge and Arika became able to morefully express both sides of her personaality, and thus is a handy freind and good girl to have in a fight for Team Umbra and the J-Team as a whole. She's usually friendly, cheerful, and level headed, if a bit snarky and cheeky, but the right stimuli can let loose her wilder side for good and for ill.

    Nico: the one on the right with Arika here◊, Nico is a budding teen psychic... that, ironically, didn't have much going for him besides that compared to the others. He's always been a big fan of the J-Team however, and jumped in at the chance he could finally belong with powers like his and be the hero he always wanted to be. However, the realization he was nothing special in comparison to many others - including the Team Umbra he joined and named - wore at his insecurities until he started feeling useless. Fortunately, after a Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door-themed adventure, he found himself, learned the real meaning of this whole heroism thing, and took several levels of badass - to the point he's now the co-leader of the WAAPT version of the X-Nauts and uses former leaderr Grodus as a mount◊. (He can also switch between his uniform and normal clothes with that staff for utility. :P) Personality-wise always eager and ready for action, but also easily flustered and historically hid his insecurities under a false ego.

    Robin: this guy may seem familiar◊, that's because he's Fire Emblem's own Robin◊, transplanted to the WAAPT world via the Glitch currently terrorizing Sinnoh having a fragment of itself in the past take a multuiversal road trip, ending with it corrupting the dark dragon Grima and leading him to WAAPT's world, where he loaned off his vessel Robin to another character under convoluted circumstances. Making Robin's life even more trouble is he's got all the memory of everything he did in Awakening's bad end timeline, including while being one with Grima. (Elaboration on differences in his story from his source material in relation can be found here for a ton of extra reading. However, he's gotten a lot better in the Pokemon world, finding genuine supportive friendships in Team Umbra, his mons, and the rest of the J-Team, and finds the new world he's been thrust into beautiful and amazing, if often bizarre. Personality wise, very sweet and kind, thoughtful, and commited to doing the right thing, but easily weirded out, a bit snarky, and can be stubborn (especially on his grudge against Corrin).

    But that's my P Cs! I'll drop bios on the others involved when their involvement is less spoilery. >:P Thoughts, new guys and those who needed the refresher?
