    Streamlining Pirates!

    Alright, so, to help make it easy for everyone involved to get into a unique role and make sure crews are as filled as they can be, let's go ahead and fill slots by role. Basically, what someone does on a ship that's important.

    • Ship Captain: This one's fairly self explanatory. They determine the course, they have absolute authority in combat, and they are also at the vote of the crew.

    • Quartermaster: This is the person that makes sure the people on the boat have something to eat and drink. Arguably the second most important role on the ship, side-by-side with the First Mate. Though they're often also the First Mate. The Quartermaster has the right to punish minor infractions on the ship, and is basically a mini-captain due to how important their duties are.

    • First Mate: Mostly an unofficial title, the First Mate is the Captain's eyes and ears. Also, usually their mouth and hands. They don't have the independent authority that the Quartermaster has, but when they invoke the captain's name, you'd better listen.

    • Sailing Master: The navigator. The person that makes sure that the ship doesn't hit any rocks, and that it's heading in the right direction. Must be able to at least operate Google Maps.

    • Boatswain: Also known as the Engineer, they maintain the ship and it's condition, patching holes, keeping resources stocked, and in general getting mad when people graffiti the hull.

    • Carpenter & Surgeon/Medical Officer: Previously known as the former, now known as the latter. Due to the strikingly similar tools the two professions used at the time, the Carpenter and the Surgeon were the same person. Thus where the name "Sawbones" comes from. Nowadays, he's more the person that passes out lemons to prevent scurvy and make sure nobody has a concussion from hitting their head against a door frame.

    • Master Gunner: Person in charge of the ship's weaponry. In the RP, likely the person who has the most sea-combat Pokemon.

    • Chef: Every ship needs a chef. That's just common sense. Sometimes the Quartermaster.

    • Sailor: The standard people doing the standard jobs. Basically, everyone can apply for this, since things are pretty simple. Minus desert-born characters who can't even swim (i.e. Dune).

    So, I'm gonna use Deziree as an example.

    • Deziree: Captain, Boatswain/Engineer

    Deziree has the drive to be a captain, but not the experience. If need be, she can just stick to maintaining the ship rather than the crew. She also has no medical experience, so that's a bad idea.

    Continuing, here's Dune:

    • Dune: Quartermaster, Medical Officer

    Dune is good at keeping supplies straight, as well as minor authority calls when the captain is occupied. He also knows basic and intermediate medical health, due to his background and personal interests.

    Together Dune and Deziree could fill a total of four separate roles. And, of course, any roles that aren't filled by people could also be filled by Pokemon if they're appropriately incentivized (and oversighted).
