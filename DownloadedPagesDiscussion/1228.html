    30688 Uilleam17th Oct 2016 05:27:06 PM from Bonnie Scotland Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Pining for the fjords
    Egads!
    Alright, I think it's about time I explained what I intend to do with the hypothetical Sunsyhore glitch. This won't explain absolutely everything, since discovering the truth for yourself is half the fun of any story, but it should give people the gist of my idea.

    WARNING: Parts of this may come off as disrespecting two highly respected members of the WAAPT playerbase. This is absolutely not intentional... well, mostly not intentional. While I do believe they were responsible for the discrepancy I describe below, even if indirectly, I don't hold it against them and actually hope they approve of my plot to fix it. Sincerest apologies in advance.

    First off, my meta reason for conceiving of this mini-plot in the first place.

    Let's begin at the beginning, which is a point before the Sinnoh arc had even started. During the planning phases for a second J-Team Sinnoh adventure, it was agreed that the crux of the plot would be the J-Team's struggles against a Glitch, of the sort they hadn't encountered since ye olden dayes. I believe it was Bitters who decided to have the Glitch be ▶ A, an obscure Glitch Pokemon from the Glitchmon homeland of Generation 1, and - more importantly, especially for this idea - that it would not be as actively malicious as Missingno or M' were. At the start of the arc, this idea was indeed adhered to. ▶ A did some nasty and bizarre things, sure - it's a Glitch, comes with the territory - but it had a playful demeanour overall and even at its worst seemed merely Obliviously Evil. Its first action was the heinous crime of turning Joseph's shirt red, for Chrissakes! Overall, it didn't seem like this would turn out to be a straightforward "punch the evil distorted pixels until they collapse" affair like it had been with the destructive Glitches of yore.

    This changed, however, and I can recall the exact moment it did. Don't ask me for a precise date - I'm not good with that sort of thing - but at some point, RP!Tagg and RP!Pent got together in the Velvet Room to privately express concern over how casually the younguns were treating the Glitch threat. This was justifiable IRP as the last two shell-shocked veterans of those earliest battles getting all PTSD over the prospect of another opponent in that vein, but it also had an obvious meta purpose - the last two survivors of the Forum Games days, who are thus the venerable and revered elders of our community, stating through the textual equivalent of an elbow in the playerbase's side and a coarse whisper in their ear that they didn't like the direction the arc was headed in. I can't tell if there was any discussion over it, since it's been many months since I could access the chat, but immediately afterwards there was an abrupt change in ▶ A's behaviour. It went in a second from a prankster that didn't realise how potentially dangerous it was to a force of destruction that purposely did shit like turning Gardenia into a man-eating plant monster. In other words, it became the second coming of Missingno or M', and I'm 99.9% sure that was exactly what Tagg and Pent had wanted - even though everyone agreed long before then that it wouldn't be like that at all. Maybe no-one else even noticed, but for me, this sudden swerve was irritating, even more so because there was absolutely zero IRP explanation. Hell, with more innocent aspects of the Glitch vanishing into thin air (anyone remember Sua?), it's as if ▶ A was retconned to have been an evil, remorseless beast from the start.

    I am not the kind of guy with whom abrupt changes sit well, especially unexplained or vaguely explained ones. Call it my inner Aspie throwing a shit-fit or something, but I've been compelled for a while to explain how this change in behaviour came about. The Sunyshore glitch, then, is intended to reveal exactly what happened to turn early-arc ▶ A into current ▶ A. Naturally, that's a damn big responsibility that would affect the whole arc, hence my seeking approval. It is worth noting that the Sunyshore glitch would be neither past nor present behaviour-wise. I won't give away exactly how it behaves, or the eventual explanation for the change, due to a desire not to spoil the story I've planned. I will, however, reveal that it blends meta decisions and fictional occurrences in typical WAAPT fashion and would potentially set up other plotlines of mine - and a new recurring foe for the J-Team, for that matter. I'm also willing to PM one person the spoilery details in advance should this make you all dubious enough to deem it necessary - probably Tagg, for reasons that should be obvious if you've read this far.

    Right, any thoughts?
