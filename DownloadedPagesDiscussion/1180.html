    Double post because me and Tangent found something more easygoing for WAAPT game nights in addition to Verax's upcoming D&D thing (which I still hope to get in on depending on my schedule, if it's not too late).

    My sister got me a game she and my aunt and uncle thought I'd like, and we played it with the rest of the family tonight and had a lot of fun. It's called Dixit; if you haven't heard of it before the gameplay on paper sounds like a whimsical Apples To Apples or Cards Against Humanity clone but in practice inverts the "everyone appeals to a judge" formula of those two and turns everybody against each other with zany guesswork and mindgames. :P

    Basic rules:

    • There's a scoreboard with figures leading to a thirty-point goal on it that the players advance on as they get points
    • There's a heckuva lot of cards, but none have text or are divided into separate categories, just all have surrealistic imagery that looks like things out of a children's book written on LSD
    • Everybody gets six cards at a time, hidden from eveyone else.
    • Each turn there's a judge figure, but unlike At A or CAH when the judge picks one of their own cards they don't reveal what it is, just give a hint and place it face-down in front of them. The hint can be anything from a word to a phrase to a sentence to a quote from something, but for the judge's best strategic opportunity the hint should neither be too obvious or too vauge and I'll get into why later.
    • Everyone else places a card on top of the judge's they feel also fits the hint, as to throw the others off.
    • The judge then shuffles the cards and reveals them, and everyone has to place bets on which was the judge's based on their hint, since all you'll know is which card was yours.
    • Once the judge's card is revealed is where we get into scoring:
      • If the judge is too obvious and everyone picks their card right, they get zilch points, everyone else does.
      • If judge is too vauge and everyone picks wrong they also get zilch points and points are assigned to those who threw people off.
      • If some people guess correctly but the others don't, however, the judge gets points and points among the other players are more complicated. (Best-case scenario for the judge is only one person gets it right).
    • Either first person to 30 points or whoever has the most points when cards run out wins.

    So tl;dr it trades At A and CAH's haggling system for everyone trying to out-guess and out-gambit each other. >:P

    I told Tangent about it and mentioned the wish to somehow create a digitized version we WAAP Ters could enjoy together, and lo and behold someone made a Tabletop Simulator mod! Me and Tangent screwed around with it a bit◊ and while I'm still learning Tabletop Simulator's controls the mod's got everything we need for the game, so if people are open would this sound like a fun option for game nights?
