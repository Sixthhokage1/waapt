    Alright, here's that dump of pics from my visit to my local zoo a few days ago.

    Which zoo is that?

    The Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden! It's the second-oldest zoo in America (been around since the 1800s and is only a few days younger than the Philadelphia Zoo; its reptile house is the oldest still-standing zoo building in America) has retooled pretty heavily around conservation efforts in recent decades, and is a staple attraction of the area; I've been there for plenty of school and family visits over the years and eventually did volunteer work there (I have plenty of amusing stories of wrangling goats, sheep, and chickens at the petting zoo), but this was my first visit in a long while and I made sure to take plenty of pics:

    Creature Features

    • To start off, the statue in front of the zoo decicated to its emblematic rhinos◊. (The Cincinnati Zoo was one of the only zoos to get anywhere with a disastrous failure of a desperate country-wide attempt at breeding more of the critically-endangered Sumatran Rhino, and even that was plagued with setbacks. On a more positive note their two remaining rhinos born at the zoo, Andalas and Harapan, have been taken back to their natural range and are acclimating to life in the wild nicely.)

    • The zoo has a similar statue of Martha, the last passenger pigeon◊, who died there in 1918. It's right next to a memorial shrine for both her species and the Carolina Parakeet, the last of which also lived at the zoo before his death.

    • On a much more positive note... BABY◊ CHEETAHS◊. They are precious.

    • Potentially a proud mama or papa cheetah◊, I'm not sure.

    • Lionesses◊. These two◊ were louging around playing with a stick together in the most cat-y fashion and it was adorable.

    • Masai◊ giraffes◊, AKA the biggest, tallest giraffes out there. They can certainly strut their stuff◊.

    • Flamingos◊, including EXTREME FLAMINGO CLOSEUP◊. That one was at a stand with this lady and her duck◊ who were teaching people how flamingos filter-feed (it's only barely vsible in the background but there was a kiddie pool full of brine shrimp several other flamingos were slurping down).

    • Gazelle, ostriches, and another antelope I can't remember the name of◊. (No Shakira among the firstmost).

    • One of the meerkats◊ peeking out of their burrow maze.

    • More ostriches◊ and a crane◊.

    • African painted◊/wild dogs◊! These guys were cute - always sticking together and running and sniffing around their enclosure. (On a Pokemon-y note I realized seeing them that close their facial markings remind me a lot of Lucario's).

    • Area where the cheetahs get their exercise◊, plus a guide to respecting one's feline overlords◊.

    • Jackass penguins◊, including one in its awkward teenage stage.

    • On that note, adolescent little blue penguins◊.

    • A bongo◊. No, seriously, that's what they're called.

    • I recognized these babydoll◊ sheep◊ I worked with years ago volunteering at the zoo; they've gotten so big!

    • American crocodile◊. Notably rarer than the alligators.

    • Baby manatee and its mama◊. :3

    • Guide to manatees and other such creatures◊.

    • This may look like a fish◊, but it's a bison amphibian called a siren!

    • Black◊ Rhinos◊.

    • More lionesses◊, except these are of an extinct-in-the wild variety with blonde or white fur; on another Pokemon-y note potential inspiration for the Sun mascot!

    • Polar bears◊! Not sure why one is brown, though.

    • This orangutan◊ looks like he could be a blackjack dealer; right there though he just seems to be contemplating all the freaky-looking ourangutan-y creatures looking at him funny.

    • Sifaka◊ lemurs◊.

    • And finally a bouncing◊ bunch◊ of bonobos◊.

    But it was a fun trip and I wanted to share with you guys! Thoughts?
