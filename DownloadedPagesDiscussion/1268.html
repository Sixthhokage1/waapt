    Possibly. At the very least, it's an option.

    Anyway!

    I'm gonna be using visuals to help assist my explanation, because words fail me at the best of times and I'm sure they fail other people occasionally as well. Also, important spots will be bolded for easier recognition.

    As it stands, the Sun/Moon timeline looks more or less like this. In summary:

    ~BEGIN ACT I~

    Protagonist starts in Melemele, butts skulls with Team Skull a bit, goes to Akala after their first Grand Trial, encounters Team Skull a bit more, and then after their second Grand Trial, visits Aether for the first time. There, they encounter the first Ultra Beast of the game, a single Nihilego. It quickly disappears, and unbeknownst to the protagonist, Lusamine begins to put her plans into action.

    ~END OF ACT I~
    ~BEGIN ACT II~

    The protagonist goes to Ula'ula Island, intending to continue their trials. Team Skull is still at it, and ultimately the protagonist faces off with them in Po Town, in what happens to be a distraction to whisk Lillie and Nebby away to get caught in the family feud. In a rush, Nanu gives the protagonist their third Grand Trial, and then they scurry on over to Aether a second time. Team Skull turns out to be working with them (gasp), family ties are made apparent, and Lusamine's madness comes to its forefront, when she uses Nebby's power to force the Ultra Wormholes open and unleash Ultra Beasts all over the region. With the Beasts loose, she and Guzma enter the wormhole and disappear, leaving the protagonist and friends in shock.

    ~END OF ACT II~
    ~BEGIN ACT III~

    Determined to pursue Lusamine and Guzma, the protagonist and Lillie hurry on to Poni Island with one Flute Of Plot Powers in hand and search for the second Flute Of Plot Powers. The protagonist has their fourth and final Grand Trial, then ventures on to Vast Poni Canyon, where their captainless seventh trial awaits — and immediately after, the Altar of the Sunne and Moone. Plot devices in hand, they finish the ritual to evolve Nebby into the Box Legendary, who opens an Ultra Wormhole at the peak. They venture forth, meet Guzma ("Y'all are stupid!"), and continue on to have their final encounter with Lusamine. Shit goes down. It's terrifying. People almost die. Lusamine goes comatose after being released from Nihilego's poisons. They then return to their own world.

    ~END OF ACT III~
    ~BEGIN ACT IV~

    With all trials finished, the protagonist has nothing left but to face the League. At the base of Mount Lanakila, they have a final battle with Gladion. At the top, they have one with Hau. They then enter the league, where — in quick succession — they must battle Hala, Olivia, Acerola, Kahili, and — at the very summit, in the Champion's Room where all of Ula'ula is visible — Kukui. They win (presumably) and become the first Champion of Alola, and a festival in honor of their triumph is held in Iki Town on Melemele. Of course, they sneak away with Lillie to meet with Tapu Koko while everyone is distracted, and Tapu Koko places its final judgment. Whatever that entails. They return and finish with the festival, and then say goodbyes as Lillie leaves to head to Kanto.

    ~END OF ACT IV~
    ~BEGIN ACT V~

    After all is said and done, there remains one final task to complete. Looker gives the new Champion an invitation to a motel room, where he and Anabel discuss the issue of the Ultra Beasts that have been roaming for a while. Now that they're not busy saving the world and becoming famous and whatnot, they can finally help seek out and detain the Ultra Beasts. In the process, Anabel and Looker's history is gradually revealed. However, the plot ends with relatively little struggle, and they finally part ways. All is done.

    ~END OF ACT V~

    Now, with that all elaborated on, I'd like to propose a timeline in which the J-Team shows up. In this example, they make it to Melemele just as the protagonist is visiting Aether for the first time — in other words, the J-Team's Act I starts when the protagonist's Act I ends and their Act II begins. Team Skull is still around to mess with the J-Team, there's been time to adjust the trial system for older members, and by the time the J-Team even hears that something might be wrong with Aether, it's already too late to get involved — Lusamine and Guzma are already off in another dimension, and pursuing them is ''literally' in the protagonist and Lillie's hands in the form of the flutes. There's only two of them, remember, and the J-Team is still an island behind anyway and quite possibly dealing with their own problems in whatever additional original plot we come up with.

    But!

    Remember that even though in canon the Ultra Beasts aren't addressed beyond Nihilego until an optional postgame plot, in the WAAPT universe they're a threat that the J-Team can take care of immediately, and I don't see any reason we shouldn't. It's optional per player, of course, and I don't see it going well if we try to follow the each-Beast-in-succession format of what would be the postgame plot, but still, we can provide our own explanation for why the protagonist actually has the freedom to go to the altar while the Beasts are still roaming unaffected. Looker and Anabel can contact the J-Team immediately when it becomes an issue, and if possible, I could also have it be explained that they're investigating on their own between the appearance of the first Nihilego and the mass release of the other Ultra Beasts.

    Anyway, with that being dealt with by others, the protagonist slips unnoticed through Ula'ula to the League, where either they or, as I proposed earlier, Hau becomes the first Champion. Or, heck, maybe even Gladion is the one who becomes the official Champion, I dunno, we still have time to figure out which one of them. Whoever it is, though, they become official before the J-Team even reaches Poni Island. Now canon is set, and we don't have to worry about being "concurrent" anymore, we can continue as we want.

    Again, this is all just a suggestion for an actual timeline structure. It's not a plot, just a frame to put it in, and if you don't want it then we don't have to keep it. I'll answer any questions but I don't want to start another argument about it.

    Right, here are some of my stupid ideas for Alolan stuff, with an emphasis on villains. None of these are anywhere near central plots (FTR, I'm currently leaning in favour of a combination of hunting the Ultra Beasts and stopping Team Cid from capturing the Zygarde parts for a main plot), but they're still ideas, aren't they?

    Note that all these ideas had their genesis while I was under the impression the arc taking place after the games was, as usual, a given. Also note that I won't necessarily do all these things at once, and would be quite willing to adjust to account for other's ideas.

    • The Aether Foundation have reformed now, of course, but an evil element still exists, in the form of two ex-Scientists named Brooke and Isaac (names provisional). Brooke and Isaac spent Aether's phase as an evil organisation assigned to work in ultra-close proximity to the Nihilego, which resulted in them receiving regular and enormous doses of the creature's venom; after Aether's HeelFace Turn, they were fired due to their obviously unstable and Nihilego-influenced behaviour. Unfortunately, by then they had been so heavily dosed with the venom that they became addicted to it, and now they work alongside a small cadre of fellow ex-Aether venom addicts to try and re-enact Lusamine's plot, all so they can get their fix. As the arc progressed, with any Nihilego they did manage to find being snapped up by the J-Team before they could use them, Brooke and Isaac would get progressively more aggressive and insane as they suffered the effects of going "cold turkey". Basically, they'd be the Neo Plasma to the Foundation's Old Plasma, but much less organised.

    • As I've said before, Skull are still skulking in Po Town, but with Guzma under Hala's close supervision and Plumeria wanting nothing to do with them anymore, they lack leadership. For now, the Grunts answer to a mysterious Trainer identified only as the "Mayor of Po Town" - a title she doesn't actually hold, but she's a strong enough Trainer that the weak Grunts are generally forced to tow her line. Behind close doors, they universally dislike her and wish their Big Bad Boss and Big Sister would come back already. [SPOILER] The Mayor is a Faller version of Clarice Voynich, hailing from a universe where it was Wilhelm who suffered the psychic injury that rendered her a psychotic mess in the main 'verse. As a result, Wilhelm died young while Clarice went on to live a fairly ordinary life - "fairly" because she fell in with a rough crowd and ended up as part of a street gang, using her psychic powers to scare their opponents into submission. Beyond the shock of suddenly jumping between universes and regions, adapting this skillset to make Skull submit to her was a breeze. Despite what all that might imply, she's not really villainous - kind of a jerk, to be sure, but ultimately she's just a directionless youngster with nothing left but a life of crime, similar to everyone else in Team Skull. [/SPOILER]

    • If Cid is taking an interest in Zygarde, it's quite possible another villainous group - namely, NI - could take interest in the Ultra Beasts. This could easily result in the J-Team facing different villains based on what they're hunting for at the time.

    • The Team Aqua characters I made a while back but who never did anything could finally be given roles, since this is a Hawaii-based region. What exactly they'd do, I'm not sure, but these guys are even less villainous and competent than Team Skull, so no worries about anything really big.
