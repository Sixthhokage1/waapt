    Egads!
    It's more action movie-themed, from what I can see, but that's hardly relevant. Still...

    If your previous FPS experiences were with "conventional" shooters, eg COD or Counter-Strike, go Soldier: 76, who simultaneously homages and parodies their playstyle. Heck, his ultimate move is a literal bloody aimbot!

    For TF2, depending on your preferred class:

    • Scout - Tracer, who has the same fast and frail design with time-manipulating powers to boot. Cheers, luv!

    • Soldier: Not Soldier: 76, funnily enough, but Pharah, the rocket launcher-wielding ex-military woman. Unlike Soldier, she doesn't even need rocket jumping to swoop in from above, thanks to her jetpack.

    • Pyro - Mmmm, tough one. Maybe Reaper, since he's also a close-ranged ambush predator.

    • Demoman - If standard, Junkrat, whose overall approach is almost identical. If Demoknight, Reinhardt, a knight-themed warrior who shares Demoknight's charge attack and emphasis on melee combat; however, Rein will spend most of his time lumbering along as his team crouch behind his Big Glowing Shield O' Doom instead of fighting directly, which TF2 has no equivalent scenario to.

    • Heavy - Most any tank, but Roadhog, being a lumbering, primarily attacking character with a powerful self-healing ability, is the most obvious choice. In fact, Roadhog is quite easy in general (my brother calls him "Noobhog").

    • Engineer - Torbjörn. Again, an obvious one, but expect chat rage when you get Play of the Game by just staring at the wall as your turret tears shit up on the other side of the map. Alternatively, play the notoriously noob-friendly Bastion and be the turret!

    • Medic - Mercy may as well be Medic with boobs and metal wings. She even speaks Gratuitous German! If you want a more unique healing experience, give the group-oriented and surprisingly damaging Lúcio a whirl.

    • Sniper - Widowmaker. 'Nuff said. Bow-wielding Hanzo approximates a Huntsman Sniper's radically different approach, but again, he is notoriously hard to master - every aspect of Hanzo requires flawless timing.

    • Spy - Another tough one. McCree, whose weapon is similar in functionality, miiiight count; overall, he's easy enough and another recommended pick for teh newbs in general.

    ...that was probably too long, but meh. I tend to pontificate on things I love. :P
    Onward to adventure, my comrades in arms!

    In my opinion, Pyros should probably pick up Mei. Ironically, despite the fact that she is the conceptual opposite of a pyromaniac, her Endothermic Blaster works very similarly to a dual Flamethrower/Flare Gun. In order to freeze enemies, she also needs to find a way into close range, which makes her play a bit more like Pyro than other character-even her abilities to create Ice Walls, fire icicles, and recover damage by turning into a living popsicle do not particularly change that. Spy has no equivalent, though. Mc Cree uses a revolver, but besides that he is not very similar-there is no real subterfuge oriented character in this game.

    One of my friends is a competitive Spy, and the fact that he's playing as Genji for reasons besides Spy (he apparently likes reflecting projectiles as Pyro, which Genji can do) sort of helps illustrate that.
