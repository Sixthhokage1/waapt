    Tangent and I have finally formulated our response to the events of the past two days: 

    The Facts of the Case

    • In an upset, Donald Trump has won election to the Presidency of the United States.
    • Donald has a history of demagoguery against minorities, and the party behind him has a platform calling for the overturn of multiple key civil rights rulings.
    • Globally, Trump has called for pulling from NATO and the Pacific, and leaving America's allies to fend for themselves, which if it comes to pass may lead to greater global instability.
    • Even now, those emboldened by his victory have begun to commit hate crimes and general harassment of queer people and PoC, both reported and unreported.
    • Many members of the RP playerbase fall under the targeted groups, and have reasonable concerns over how the country may change over the next few years and how it might affect them, especially those living in conservative-dominated areas.
      • In the near immediate aftermath, Bittersweet noted hearing Trump voters/supporters yelling slogans nearby their dorm, and their roommate just so happens to be the president of their college's LGBTQ+ club, leaving them in a very precarious situation.
      • Every has mentioned their friends telling them that during an anti-Trump rally held in Manhattan this evening gone violent due to the interference of Trump supporters, one person was hit by a bottle thrown by a Trump supporter.
      • Keys mentioned his front lawn getting vandalized because of the Vote Hillary sign he had on it, and fears his family possibly getting attacked for his voting choices. He has also mentioned one case of the reverse in his neighborhood, Hillary supporters throwing rocks at the house of a neighbor. (Who also supported Hillary, so their actions don't make even the slightest modicum of sense.), but from what I can tell nationally at least it would appear there are more Trump supporters behaving badly overall than Hillary supporters.
    • Even for those not in targeted groups, Trump's economic policies are questionable at best, and an Obamacare repeal is likely.
      • Nuclear war is not a probable concern. 

    What to do Now

    • For sanity and safety, we need support networks, including this community.
      • Help each other through this time.
      • Practice self-care. Don't let worry over potential possibilities hurt your ability to deal with them.
      • Do not target people for the results of the race, especially if you know they voted differently from you.
    • Don't consider expressions of extreme sadness and fear over the results to be overreacting; while we shouldn't let our fear control us, that doesn't mean what's going on isn't extremely serious.
      • Don't dismiss people's lived experiences.
    • Longer term, if you're American, vote in future elections, including the midterm elections as they tend to be the ones most likely to flip seats in Congress. Vote in local elections too; they tend to have the most direct effect on your immediate needs and are best positioned to defend rights.
    • Keep hope. There are many good things left in the world, and new ones being created.
      • We'll overcome this together.
