    Okay, this is purely hypothetical, but I kind of wanted to post some thoughts and ideas I have had floating around.

    Basically, if I were ever to retire Jack, I would want him to be part of/head a project involving fossil pokemon. Basically, being from a family that is experienced with daycares and breeding pokemon, Jack knows first-hand how many offsprings of pokemon are just thrown out into the wild because they can't be cared for. People will breed twenty Torchics and only use two. And that's fine, to some degree. They can fend for themselves, because they're generally released into the wild where they're native to (although not always).

    However, Fossil Pokemon are not native to any environment. Their existence in this world is completely artificial and have a hard time surviving anywhere, let alone thriving. This is where the project comes in. It's essentially Jurassic Park, but not an amusement park.

    Theoretically, there could be an island that rose out of the ocean recently, due to volcanic eruption. Hoenn is a perfect place for that to happen, for obvious reasons. Jack and a few others with some assets and cash could purchase/lease/license the island and set up the project.

    They could research what kind of environment would suit the fossil pokemon the best. What species of plants to introduce and stuff like that. Ones that have been around for a long time, such as ferns and magnolia trees would be prime candidates. Other pokemon that have been around since they've been alive too, like Yanmega and Feraligatr.

    This could exist eventually without human interaction or intervention and be a great place for fossil pokemon to exist in their natural habitat without them having a natural habitat.

    On one more note, they would mostly be offspring of fossil pokemon and not directly revived, because directly revived fossil pokemon tend to not do well even in ideal conditions and aren't aware of The Vow between Pokemon and Humans.
