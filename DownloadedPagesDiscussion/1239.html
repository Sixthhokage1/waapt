    I will now attempt to help bridge an ideological gap.

    There are a lot of reasons people are upset about this election. I will now attempt to identify two of them.

    People were concerned about the electors themselves. The Presidency of the United States is a powerful position, and the person holding that position is potentially very influential.

    However, and this might be the crux of the problem, people are concerned about how the elector will color the ideology and attitudes of their supporters, especially with the rampant partisanship that was demonstrated during the Obama years. Completely independent of either elector and their politics, people who view themselves as supporters of the winning side will feel vindicated, will feel as if the country is now theirs.

    That is a very dangerous feeling.

    This is a very dangerous feeling because people will build a romanticized version of their political party based around their own beliefs rather than those demonstrated by the actual politics. Both parties are guilty of this, ultimately - "We will make sure everyone in America has affordable health care!" (pharmaceutical companies who don't want to play ball spike prices, causing backorders and making hospital visits hell). "Unrestricted access to firearms to EVERYONE is a right of the constitution!" (violent gun crime continues to dominate headlines).

    But the reason this particular community feels so strongly about this election has already been highlighted this year, in Orlando.

    And honestly, I can't blame either side for feeling how they do.

    I'm just tired.
