I miss him a lot and it contributed to me being really depressed around then, and the guys in charge of my apartment complex don't think they'll be able to find a suitable replacement anytime in the forseeable future, but there's good news to all this, and half of it is they moved me into his more spacious old room.

    ...Oh wait. I forgot to mention what we did with my old one.

    ...BEHOLD THE NERD LAIR◊! As that establishing shot demonstrates, I made a whole museum of my geeky memorabilia out of my old room and it's magical.

    Further◊ shots◊ showcase different parts of the room, with closeups of note:

    • The entertainment center◊ with a good chunk of my video games and home consoles. (Specifically my Xbox 360, Gamecube, and Wii U for the latter). Also a DS Phat in the corner.

    • What I have dubbed the Nintentable◊, featuring figurines/keychains/buttons/etc of various characters from franchises published by Nintendo. Naturally, Pokemon has its own sizable section, but much of the rest of Nintendo's video game pantheon is present as well. Note the amiibo - Smash collection front and center with others right in the middle just behind.

    • A special little display◊ below the Nintentable for Brawl in the Family.     • A little section◊ for non-Nintendo but still nerdy keepsakes of various sorts.     • A poster◊ from the Pokemon Symphonic Evolutions concert I went to with my mom, sister, and a good RL friend last year! Charmander's rocking that flute.

    Pokesona Player Alert

    Other than Pokesona dungeons running longer than I anticipated - which is problem that was caused more by me holding everyone to standards they probably aren't comfortable dealing with so that is my bad. But beyond that I've noticed a few people who shall remain nameless that joined dungeon runs but stopped posting for them.

    Now I know as well as anyone here that RL things do come up and that there are things more important than the RP, but I think more consistency is needed.

    So for future reference if you know you won't be able to post a response at least once a day when a dungeon's plot advances, you should consider sitting that dungeon out. But if you do decided to join a dungeon adventure, please be sure that you post consistently during that dungeon.

    However, even I know that unexpected events happen. If for some reason you can't consistently post for a Dungeon after joining the group for it, please leave your PC(s) in the control of someone you trust to do so. Or at least alert the Dungeon's GM and the plotrunners that you need to drop out.

    But if you just stop posting responses for a dungeon for an extended period of time with no warning and no notice as to why you stopped too many times - or just not participating at all - You WILL be dropped out of getting the participation bonus.
