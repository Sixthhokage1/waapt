If anything I was thinking "Nando 2.0" when cementing Ren's character arc.

    • I DID salvage all I could. Unfortunately Ren had already retired, so without some really fancy writing that I don't think I could pull off alone - and even now I doubt I can do it without at least one retcon - and I was told "you can only do that kind of super-big stuff in your retirement post" there was no point in keeping the map I had set out; since I had actually asked about doing it on the chat and gotten a lot of approval. Then you came along and said no then started getting some of the support I had gotten to veto my idea - even after I explained it all.
      • As a note - as this will be fully laid out below - the GF thing WAS the rebound for an earlier failure. The Diantha bit was to ensure Ren's head didn't swell and to have something to crawl back up towards and give reason to Ren's character arc. And if I did have Ren lose both as you gave the example of, Ren would have entered a serious BSOD that would have lasted years. So yes, within the limits I was given at the time of the veto, I've done all that I've can to keep Ren going while still trying get him to be an interesting character.

    • Well in retrospect, it DID come off childish, however, since the veto form the past was basically under the reasoning:
      • "It's too sue-ish"
        • Unless I'm totally off base here, the sue-ish trait you all were most likely referring to is "a character who is able to do anything with with little to no experience and with little to no effort". Ren had around 11 years (2003-2014) of contest experience by that point in the RP, so It wasn't sue-ish if he just wanted to do something he'd tried and failed to do when he was younger but couldn't do because of lack of experience. This is especially true because of the stuff I mentioned above. At the time I likened this to an Olympic medal-winning athlete going all out and winning four gold medals in their last Olympic games before they planned to retire.

      • "PCs can't do big stuff like this happen IRP otherwise the might become too OP and cause problems with canon and IRP power levels." So I was told to wait until my retirement post to do it.
        • This was the reason I was actually able to accept the judgement. Because after a long and careful thought on it, it made sense. And this is why when I've seen other ideas with similar scale be shot down (and then reworked) I accepted that that was how the RP worked.

    So when I saw everyone supporting the person-who-spearheaded-the-veto-of-my-idea's idea, of course I was going to call it out. As I felt it was not only not fair to me, but to any others whose ideas where shot down in the past. Because I certainty hadn't heard about a change in stance on the topic of "PCs Achieving Big Things".
