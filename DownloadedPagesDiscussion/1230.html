    So, I said, I'd discuss when I got back, and back I finally am. God it feels good to have a keyboard again.

    So! What happened?

    Well, a couple of you may know about an incident I had about a month back where I passed out for a brief moment in a choir rehearsal . Well, I didn't really think about it, but 3-4 more times while I was in choir rehearsals, I got dizzy and passed out.

    Well, since about a couple of weeks ago, I've been having a lot of really bad nosebleeds. Now, for this, I still was not super concerned, I usually have nosebleeds this time of year.

    But Tuesday, as I was fixing to take a shower, I started having a nosebleed, so I had to wait for it to stop for some time. Well, in doing so, I got lightheaded, and thinking it was because of the steam and heat, I cut off the shower and left the room. I sat down in the chair, and the next thing I remember I was picking my head up off the table.

    So, yeah, freaking out, I called my grandma and said that I needed to see a doctor. We called my mom, and she called my doctor, who recommended that I see the ER, in case they needed to cauterize my nose. So, we went up there, and about 5 hours later, I finally got to see a doctor. The doctor recommended I stay overnight to see what the deal was, and I begrudgingly started being put through a series of blood tests.

    Well, they quickly ruled out Anemia, low blood pressure, irregular heartrate and low blood sugar, so they did an Echo-cardiogram on my heart, which showed nothing abnormal. So they did a bubble-stress-test thing, where they agitated some blood and saline solution to see how the bubbles dispersed, which FINALLY showed them something.

    Apparently I have HHT, which is where the capillaries in between the artery and veins going between my heart and lungs are either non-existent or not working, so blood doesn't oxygenate properly. Minor, but can be lethal if untreated. So I'll be seeing a doctor for that for the rest of my life.

    In addition, because of my late sister's aneurysm, they ran an MRI on me, and found a cyst on the base of my brain, and a nodule on my pituitary gland. So yeah. Fun. I'm gonna have an intimate relationship with way more doctors than I was hoping to have to deal with for quite some time. FML.
