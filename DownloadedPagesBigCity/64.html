    Li'l Punk
    Kori tried to edge around Gallade, only to be held back by Tuft.

    "Let 'im 'ave 'is momen'," he said softly. "'E'll be much easier to deal wit'."

    Kori glanced at Leo, who nodded knowingly, then shrugged and stood back, arms crossed over her chest.

    Gallade stepped into a circle of Pokemon. There were all different types, from an enormous Yanmega hovering in the shadows to a Spinda sitting off to the side.

    "My friends," he said darkly, his hat pulled low over his face. Kori noticed a few Pokemon off to the side whispering, their eyes on the hat. She guessed they were wondering the same thing she had earlier; when it would inevitably fall off.

    "I bring with me a newcomer to our... investigation." With this, he stepped to the side, flicking one hand to allow his coat to billow out to the side as he did so. A little impressed despite herself, Kori stepped into the circle at a nudge from Tuft and looked around at the watching agents.

    "Uh, hi. I'm Kori."

    A Noctowl at the head of the circle gave her a small salute with his wing, and Kori remembered Tuft saying his father was here.

    "So, I found some of the candies in Thorn Gang territories. No addicts, though, 'cause I was kinda distracted and Leo wouldn't tell me what an addict looked like." She shrugged, self-consciously scratching an arm.

    "Thank you, Kori," Gallade closed in on her, looking down. "Now, if you would..."

    "Oh! Right!" Kori gave him the candy. "Now, um, I have somewhere to be. I kinda promised my friends I'd be with them in Fight gang territory, so..." She began edging away from Gallade. The tall Pokemon furrowed his brows, thinking over what she just said.

    The Noctowl glanced between her and Gallade quickly, then stepped forward.

    "Undercover work! Excellent! A quality find, Boss," he said, fast and loud.

    "Yes, of course. I commend you," Gallade nodded slowly to Kori, backing off. "Begin your mission with all haste."

    "Oh, uh, of course." A little confused, Kori Quick Attacked down the alley.

    Low murmurs followed her, and she was still confused when she felt a light tap on her shoulder. Spinning around with an Ice Punch held up in front of her face, she was surprised to see the Noctowl.

    "Whoa! Easy, there. I need to talk to you."

    "What about?" Kori asked suspiciously, lowering her attack.

    "What you just did back there... that was risky. No one just quits on the Boss, kid. You're lucky I stepped up." Noctowl looked at her warningly. "Be more careful."

    "Oh, uh, thanks, then. But... how am I supposed to get out if I can't quit?" Kori sounded slightly worried, her mind moving to Jet and Absol.

    "You tell someone. Tell Leo, he's a dependable guy, if completely devoted to the Boss. We'll help you out. Just don't say it outright. Boss might seem all nice and a little silly on the outside, but he's dead serious about this job."

    "All right. Thanks." Kori started backing up, her Ice Punch completely dissipated.

    "Go on, kid. And if you ever need me, or Tuft, or Leo, or anybody, just find a mirror and flash it into the air. Someone will show up. Anyone working with the Boss will help you out, related to the mission or not." Noctowl smiled at her, then took off.

    "Okay. Bye! Thanks!" Kori took off, Quick Attacking towards the park Absol had told her to go to.

    Absol followed Kori inside, nudging the Sneasel through the doors. He looked at her. "Go find some of your friends. I'll get Lucario."

Every kind apparently made their home here, most of them exchanging blows, exercising, or merely watching the proceedings. Two combatants in particular drew the Buizel's attention.

    It was a Lopunny fighting a Combusken, though the fire chicken wasn't the one that interested Jet. The Lopunny's looks alone marked him out as odd.

    Instead of being the characteristic puffballs, the fur on the ends of the rabbit's long ears were limp and haggard, the same with every bit of poofy fur on him. As Jet watched, the Lopunny shot into the air, bounced off of a wall, and came back towards the Combusken, a Thunder Punch building up around one fist.

    The Combusken reacted with practiced ease, countering the Lopunny's jump with one of his own, his claw glowing blue in a Sky Uppercut that veered upward into the Lopunny's cheek. The brown fur and the skin beneath it rolled up and tilted the Normal-type's head to the side before finally throwing him through the air, slamming him against a wall to land on his hands and knees on the ground.

    Combusken came back to earth and nodded. "I think that's enough for today, Hamlet."

    Jet memorized the name and look of the Lopunny, then turned around and saw Absol entering with Kori. He ran over to the Sneasel. "So, how'd it go?"

    Jet led Kori into the room that he'd seen Hamlet in, looking around for a bit before spotting the Lopunny nearby. The Normal-type had a look of intense concentration on his face as he sent various attacks into a punching bag. "That's him. His name's Hamlet, from what I've heard."

    Meanwhile, Absol and Lucario walked down a hallway, far up the building, above any of the sleeping and training areas.

    "So you really think that there's more to this Rare Candy thing than meets the eye?" Lucario asked.

    Absol nodded. "None of my contacts have seen Razor since he fled. You would think that he'd be building up a new gang, but he hasn't been seen at all in the past few days. It's as if he left the city entirely."

    "To go into the wild?" Lucario asked. "Strange, for a gang leader to not try and get back territory."

    "And then there was this group of Scythers and Scizors that arrived recently, as well. They've taken over the Thorn Gang. I found Roserade a while ago. They killed her."

    "But what does that have to do with the Rare Candy trade?" Lucario asked.

    "It's possible that either disrupting the trafficking or controlling it in some way would be conductive to destabilizing the balance of power in the city and turning all of the gangs against one another. I doubt that someone would do that for amusement."

    "So you're saying there's an outside force at work."

    "Exactly."

    Lucario raised an eyebrow at the Dark-type. "Are you certain?"

    "Not quite, but there's strong evidence. I'll have to do some more research, maybe get in contact with this Gallade that Kori mentioned. If something has plans for the city, all of the gangs need to be working together, not at one another's throats."

    Lucario nodded. "Alright. Go do what you need to do. I'll look after your friends."

    Absol smiled. "Thank you, Lucario. I owe you one."

    Lucario grinned. "Just go, don't get all sappy on me."

    Absol turned and ran back down the stairs, exiting the building via a back way.
